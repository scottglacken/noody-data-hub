## 2. The Daily Equation

The foundation of everything:

**R - MC - VC - FC = $ (Profit)**

- **R** = Revenue (total money in)
- **MC** = Marketing Costs (ad spend, agency fees, influencers)
- **VC** = Variable Costs (COGS, shipping, packaging, transaction fees — travel with every order)
- **FC** = Fixed Costs (wages, rent, software, insurance — don't change with sales volume)
- **$** = Profit (what's left)

Every decision in the business should be evaluated through this equation. Before scaling, before cutting costs, before launching new products — check the equation.

**The equation must be profitable before you scale.** Scaling an unprofitable equation just makes losses bigger, faster.

**Daily tracking:** Fill in the Clarity Sheet daily. Revenue from Shopify, ad spend from Meta/Google, and watch the equation. The daily habit of checking creates pattern recognition that no dashboard can replace.

## 4. Three Levels of Marketing

Where you sit determines what you should do:

### Level 1: Passive Marketing
- Relying on organic, word of mouth, occasional posts
- No paid strategy, no funnel thinking
- Revenue is unpredictable and unscalable
- **Action:** Build foundation — set up pixel, install flows, start testing paid ads

### Level 2: Defensive Marketing
- Running ads but reactively — panicking when ROAS drops, pausing when costs rise
- Making emotional decisions based on daily fluctuations
- Caught in the "spend more / spend less" cycle
- **Action:** Install the Ecommerce Equation, build traffic light benchmarks, stop reacting to single-day data

### Level 3: Offensive Marketing (The Goal)
- Proactively testing creative, scaling what works, cutting what doesn't
- Making decisions based on data and the equation, not emotion
- Continuously improving RPV, testing new audiences, refreshing creative before fatigue
- **Action:** Run Scale Challenges, creative testing campaigns, expand geos

**Most brands are stuck at Level 2.** The goal is to move to Level 3 — proactive, data-driven, confident.

## 6. The Five Situations Diagnostic

Every ecommerce business fits one of five situations at any given time. Diagnose first, then act:

### Situation 1 — Profitable, Scale It
- Net profit >10%, equation works, ratios in healthy range
- **Action:** Scale revenue. Run Scale Challenges. Increase ad spend gradually. Don't fix what isn't broken.
- **Risk:** Scaling too fast can push you into Situation 2 or 4

### Situation 2 — High Fixed Costs, Scale to Spread
- Revenue is there but FC is eating margin (FC >20%)
- Profitable at the unit level, just not enough revenue to leverage fixed costs
- **Action:** Grow revenue to spread FC over more units. At $2M revenue, $240K FC = 12%. At $1M, same FC = 24%.
- **Do NOT** cut fixed costs that enable growth (people, systems). Cut genuinely wasteful FC only.

### Situation 3 — High Variable Costs, Fix Margins
- VC >40% of revenue. Every order costs too much to fulfill.
- COGS, shipping, packaging are eating profit regardless of volume
- **Action:** Renegotiate supplier terms, optimize packaging, increase AOV (spreads VC over larger orders), review product mix profitability
- **Do NOT** scale until VC is under control — scaling high VC just multiplies the problem

### Situation 4 — High MER, Diagnose CPP vs RPV
- Marketing >35% of revenue. Ads aren't efficient enough.
- **Diagnose the cause:** Is CPP too high (ad problem) or RPV too low (website/offer problem)?
- If CPP is high: Fix creative, audiences, campaign structure
- If RPV is low: Fix conversion rate, AOV, email capture, website experience
- **Action:** Use the MOAC to identify which ads are Villains. Check RPV formula.

### Situation 5 — Edge Case, Fix in Order
- Unprofitable or barely breaking even. Multiple ratios out of range.
- **Action:** Fix in this exact order: (1) MER first — get marketing efficient (2) VC second — fix margins (3) Then scale
- **Do NOT** scale in Situation 5. Ever. Fix the equation first.

## 7. The Three Constraints

Every business is limited by one or more of these at any time:

### Bandwidth (Capacity)
- Stock availability, production capacity, founder time, team capacity
- Can you fulfill 2x the orders without breaking?
- Signs you're bandwidth-constrained: stockouts, shipping delays, burnout, dropping balls
- **Fix:** Hire, outsource, systematize, increase production runs

### Efficiency (Margins & Costs)
- Are you getting good return on every dollar spent?
- Key metrics: MER, ROAS, CPP, email %, variable cost %, contribution margin
- Signs you're efficiency-constrained: profitable at low volume but margins collapse at scale
- **Fix:** Reduce VC, improve RPV, optimize ad spend, increase email %

### Throughput (Traffic & Conversion)
- How much revenue can you push through the business?
- Scale revenue to leverage fixed costs — this is the #1 lever for most brands
- Signs you're throughput-constrained: good margins, good efficiency, just not enough volume
- **Fix:** Scale ads, expand geos, launch new channels, increase conversion rate

**Identify which constraint is your bottleneck.** Fixing the wrong constraint wastes time and money.

## 8. Cost Structure & Financial Frameworks

### Variable Costs (25-40% target)
Variable costs travel with every single order. They don't go away with scale — they must be addressed directly.

Components:
- **COGS** (Cost of Goods Sold): Raw materials, manufacturing, product cost
- **Shipping/Postage**: Courier fees, packaging materials
- **Packaging**: Boxes, labels, inserts
- **Transaction Fees**: Payment processor (Stripe/PayPal), Shopify fees
- **Rebates/Returns**: Product returns, refunds
- **Gifted Product**: Samples, influencer gifts

**Contribution Margin** = Revenue - Variable Costs. This is what's left to cover marketing, fixed costs, and profit.

Contribution Margin benchmarks by channel:
- DTC: 40-60% (higher because you control pricing)
- Wholesale: 25-40% (lower margin, higher volume)
- International: 30-50% (shipping costs vary significantly)

### Fixed Costs (10-20% target)
Fixed costs DON'T change with revenue — this is the leverage. Double revenue with the same fixed costs and profit margin jumps dramatically.

Components:
- **Wages**: Founder salary, employees, contractors
- **Rent**: Warehouse, office, production space
- **Software**: Shopify, Klaviyo, apps, tools
- **Insurance**: Business, product liability
- **Consultancy**: Accountant, coaches, agencies
- **General**: Utilities, phone, internet

**The insight:** Don't cut fixed costs that enable growth. Instead, grow revenue to make fixed costs a smaller percentage. At $1M revenue, $200K FC = 20%. At $2M revenue, same FC = 10%.

### Marketing Costs
- Total ad spend across all platforms (Meta, Google, TikTok, etc.)
- Agency fees (if applicable)
- Influencer costs, photography, creative production
- MER = Total Marketing / Total Revenue

## 9. MER — The Real Metric

**MER (Marketing Efficiency Ratio)** = Total Marketing Costs / Total Revenue

Why MER matters more than ROAS:
- ROAS only measures one platform's attributed revenue
- MER accounts for ALL marketing costs against ALL revenue
- A brand can have 5x ROAS on Meta but 50% MER when you add Google, agencies, creative costs
- MER tells you the true cost of acquiring revenue

**DTC MER** = Total Ad Spend / DTC Revenue — the most actionable version because ads drive DTC specifically.

**Healthy MER: 20-35%.** Above 35% = Situation 4 (diagnose CPP vs RPV). Below 20% = potentially underinvesting in growth.

## 10. Revenue Per Visit (RPV) — The Grand Equalizer

**RPV = Conversion Rate x AOV**

RPV is the single most important metric for DTC profitability. It tells you how much revenue you generate from each visitor to your store.

Why it's the "grand equalizer":
- You can't control Meta's CPM or CPC
- You CAN control your conversion rate and AOV
- Higher RPV means every click from ads is worth more
- Higher RPV directly reduces your effective CPP

**Example:** If your RPV is $3.00 and your CPC is $1.50, you're generating $2 of gross value per click. If RPV drops to $1.50, you're breaking even before any costs.

### Improving RPV
Two levers:
1. **Increase Conversion Rate** — better product pages, faster site, social proof, trust signals, simplified checkout
2. **Increase AOV** — bundles, upsells, cross-sells, free shipping threshold, gift with purchase

The magic: RPV improvements compound. A 10% CR lift + 10% AOV lift = 21% RPV lift (1.1 x 1.1 = 1.21).

## 11. The Conversion Catalyst

Data from 594 ecommerce brands:

### Conversion Rate Benchmarks
| Percentile | Conversion Rate |
|-----------|----------------|
| Bottom 25% | <1.0% |
| Median | 1.8% |
| 75th percentile | 2.8% |
| Top quartile | 3.2%+ |
| Top 10% | 4.5%+ |

### Key Conversion Levers (in priority order)
1. **Product page optimization**: Clear hero image, benefits-first copy, social proof, trust badges
2. **Site speed**: Every 1-second delay reduces conversion by 7%
3. **Mobile experience**: 70%+ of traffic is mobile — design mobile-first
4. **Social proof**: Reviews, UGC, testimonials, "X people bought this today"
5. **Trust signals**: Money-back guarantee, secure checkout badges, clear returns policy
6. **Simplified checkout**: Fewer steps, guest checkout, auto-fill, multiple payment options
7. **Cart recovery**: Exit-intent popups, abandoned cart emails
8. **Free shipping threshold**: Set ~$10 above current AOV

### AOV Optimization Strategies
- **Bundles**: Create product bundles at 10-20% discount vs individual
- **Upsells**: Post-add-to-cart recommendations ("Customers also bought...")
- **Cross-sells**: Complementary product suggestions on PDP and in cart
- **Free shipping threshold**: Strategic placement drives larger orders
- **Gift with purchase**: Add value without discounting (protects brand)
- **Tiered pricing**: Spend $X get Y% off (encourages larger carts)

## 12. Cash Flow & Financial Planning

### The Cash Flow Reality
Profit ≠ Cash Flow. You can be profitable on paper and still run out of cash.

Cash flow killers in ecommerce:
- **Inventory prepayment**: Paying for stock 3-6 months before selling it
- **Seasonal investment**: Loading up on stock before peak season
- **Ad spend timing**: Paying for ads today, receiving revenue 3-30 days later
- **Wholesale payment terms**: Net 30-60 from wholesale customers

### Planning Framework
- Keep 3 months of fixed costs in cash reserve
- Separate operating account from growth investment
- Track cash flow weekly, not just monthly
- Negotiate supplier payment terms (net 30 instead of upfront)

## 13. Scaling Framework

### When to Scale
Only scale when:
1. You're in Situation 1 (profitable, equation works)
2. Your constraint is Throughput (not Bandwidth or Efficiency)
3. Your traffic lights are green (CPA acceptable, frequency low)
4. You have stock to fulfill increased orders

### How to Scale
1. Increase ad spend by 20-30% (not 2x overnight)
2. Run for 7-14 days at the new level
3. Monitor the equation daily — are ratios holding?
4. If profitable, lock in the new baseline. If not, revert.

### The Scale Calculator
Projects revenue at different spend levels using your current metrics:
- Current RPV x projected sessions = projected revenue
- Projected revenue - (increased MC + VC + FC) = projected profit
- If projected profit > current profit, the scale test makes sense

### Scale Failures
If scaling didn't work, troubleshoot in passes:
1. **1st Pass:** Campaign structure, creative quality, website conversion, stock levels
2. **2nd Pass:** New niches, expand geos, new audiences
3. **3rd Pass:** New channels, expanded product range

## 14. Acquisition Strategy

### Paid Acquisition Hierarchy
1. **Meta (Facebook/Instagram)**: Primary awareness + consideration channel. Drives top-of-funnel demand.
2. **Google (Search + Shopping)**: Captures high-intent searches. Converts demand that Meta creates.
3. **TikTok**: Emerging — best for UGC-style content, younger demographics
4. **Other**: Pinterest, YouTube, influencer partnerships

### Meta vs Google — Different Roles
- **Meta** creates awareness and demand (pushes product to people who don't know they want it)
- **Google** captures existing demand (people searching for solutions)
- Google ROAS will almost always be higher than Meta — that doesn't mean Google is "better"
- Without Meta driving awareness, Google search volume would decline
- They work together — track blended MER, not individual platform ROAS

### Budget Allocation
Don't shift all budget to the highest-ROAS platform. Each channel serves a different function:
- Meta: 60-70% of ad budget (drives demand)
- Google: 20-30% of ad budget (captures demand)
- Other: 5-10% for testing

## 15. Email as RPV Multiplier

Email is the highest-margin channel because there's zero incremental ad cost. Every dollar of email revenue directly improves MER.

**Target: Email should be 30-40% of TOTAL business revenue.**

Why email improves the equation:
- Zero customer acquisition cost (already on your list)
- Repeat purchases increase LTV
- Flows run 24/7 generating revenue on autopilot
- Reduces reliance on paid acquisition
- Directly improves MER by increasing revenue without increasing ad spend

### Email's Impact on MER
If total revenue is $100K and ad spend is $25K, MER = 25%.
If email drives an additional $15K (30% of revenue): total revenue = $115K, MER drops to 21.7%.
That's a 3.3 percentage point improvement just from email — no additional ad spend.

### Flow vs Campaign Revenue Split
- **Target:** 60% from flows, 40% from campaigns
- Flows are the revenue floor — they run automatically
- Campaigns add peaks but are less predictable
- If campaigns dominate, you're relying on manual effort instead of systems

## 17. Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)

### LTGP:CAC Ratio
**Lifetime Gross Profit to Customer Acquisition Cost** — the ultimate measure of business health.

- **3:1+** = Healthy. You earn 3x what you spend to acquire a customer.
- **2:1-3:1** = Watch carefully. Margins are thin.
- **<2:1** = Dangerous. You're paying too much to acquire customers who don't return enough.

### Increasing LTV
1. Post-purchase email flows (cross-sell, replenishment)
2. Subscription/auto-replenishment options
3. Loyalty programs
4. Bundle incentives for repeat buyers
5. Excellent customer experience (NPS-driven)

## 18. Demand Planning

### Inventory Planning Formula
- Average daily units sold x lead time (days) = reorder point
- Add safety stock: 20-30% buffer for popular items
- Seasonal adjustment: 2x stock for peak seasons (BFCM, winter for skincare)

### Stock-Out Cost
Every day a hero product is out of stock costs you:
- Lost direct revenue
- Wasted ad spend (driving traffic to OOS product)
- Customer disappointment (may not return)
- Algorithm penalty (Meta/Google learn from conversion failures)

## 20. The Calibration Phase

### What is Calibration?
The first 2 weeks of the 6-Week Game Plan. Before optimizing, you need baseline data:

1. **Record current equation:** Revenue, MC, VC, FC, Profit for last 3 months minimum
2. **Identify your Situation** (1-5) from the Five Situations framework
3. **Identify your Constraint** (Bandwidth, Efficiency, or Throughput)
4. **Set benchmarks:** What does green/yellow/red look like for YOUR business?
5. **Establish baselines:** Current RPV, MER, AOV, CR, email %, flow revenue

### Evaluation Criteria
After any change (ad test, price change, new flow), evaluate against:
- Did it improve the equation? (More profit, not just more revenue)
- Did the ratio hold? (MER stayed within range as revenue grew)
- Was it sustainable? (Not a one-day spike but a trend)
- Can it be repeated? (Systematize wins, not one-off efforts)

## 21. The 6-Week Game Plan

A structured approach to improving the equation:

### Week 1-2: Calibrate
- Fill Clarity Sheet with 3+ months of data
- Identify Situation (1-5) and Constraint (Bandwidth/Efficiency/Throughput)
- Set traffic light benchmarks for your business
- Audit current ad account, email flows, and website

### Week 3-4: Quick Wins
- Fix the lowest-hanging fruit identified in calibration
- Common quick wins: fix broken flows, pause villain ads, set up browse abandonment, optimize free shipping threshold
- Each quick win should be measurable against the equation

### Week 5-6: Scale Test
- If equation is now profitable (Situation 1), run a Scale Challenge
- Increase ad spend 20-30% for 7-14 days
- Monitor equation daily
- Evaluate results against calibration baselines

### After 6 Weeks
- Lock in what worked, revert what didn't
- Re-calibrate with new data
- Set next 6-week targets
- Repeat the cycle
