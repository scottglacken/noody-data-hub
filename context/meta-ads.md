# Meta Ads — Noody Skincare Context

## Three Levels of Marketing
Where you sit determines what you should do:

### Level 1: Passive Marketing
- Relying on organic, word of mouth, occasional posts
- No paid strategy, no funnel thinking, revenue unpredictable
- **Action:** Build foundation — pixel, flows, start testing paid

### Level 2: Defensive Marketing
- Running ads reactively — panicking when ROAS drops, pausing when costs rise
- Emotional decisions based on daily fluctuations
- **Action:** Install the Ecommerce Equation, build traffic light benchmarks, stop reacting to single-day data

### Level 3: Offensive Marketing (The Goal)
- Proactively testing creative, scaling what works, cutting what doesn't
- Data-driven decisions based on the equation, not emotion
- **Action:** Run Scale Challenges, creative testing campaigns, expand geos

**Most brands are stuck at Level 2. The goal is Level 3 — proactive, data-driven, confident.**

## MER Is the Real Metric, Not ROAS
ROAS only measures one platform's attributed revenue. MER accounts for ALL marketing costs against ALL revenue.

**MER to ROAS Conversion:**
| MER | Equivalent ROAS |
|-----|----------------|
| 15% | 6.7x |
| 20% | 5.0x |
| 25% | 4.0x |
| 30% | 3.3x |
| 35% | 2.9x |

**Formula:** ROAS = 1 / MER. DTC MER = Total Ad Spend / DTC Revenue.

A brand can have 5x ROAS on Meta but terrible MER when you add Google, agencies, creative costs. Always check the equation.

## CPP — The Key Ad Efficiency Metric
**Cost Per Purchase (CPP)** = Amount Spent / Purchases

CPP is more actionable than ROAS because it tells you the actual cost to acquire each sale. CPP thresholds by funnel stage:
- **TOF good:** AOV x MER x 2 (cold traffic is expensive)
- **MOF good:** AOV x MER (warm retargeting)
- **BOF good:** AOV x MER x 0.75 (hot, ready to buy)

## Campaign Structure (2025 Meta)
### Three Campaign Types
- **ASC-RS**: Advantage+ Sales Campaign ON + Recommended Setup (Meta controls targeting)
- **ASC-LS**: Advantage+ ON + Limited Setup (you suggest targeting)
- **NASC-LS**: Advantage+ OFF, full control over targeting

### Budget Allocation

|-------------|----------|-------------------|
| TOF | 45% | $22.50 |

## Traffic Light System
Daily assessment tool for CPA, reach, and frequency:

**Frequency Benchmarks (14-day window):**
| Light | Frequency |
|-------|-----------|
| Green | ~1.0 |
| Yellow | ~2.0 |
| Red | 3.0+ |

**Ad Classification:**
| Type | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Hero Ad** | Highest reach, green frequency, acceptable CPA — sucks budget sustainably |
| **Sidekick** | Good CPA but limited scale (lower reach, higher frequency) |
| **Psychic** | Best CPA in campaign but may not reach as many people |
| **Turn Off** | Red CPA and poor reach/frequency — turn off immediately |

## Creative Testing
- Always have 3-5 active creative tests running
- Hero ad identification: high reach + low frequency + acceptable CPA sustained over 14+ days
- **Fatigue detection:** CPA rising across date range comparisons (14d → 7d → 3-4d) = creative fatigue
- **Refresh cadence:** Replace fatigued creative every 2-3 weeks before performance collapses
- Best performers for Noody: UGC testimonials from NZ parents, before/after skin results, product-in-use lifestyle

## Revenue Per Visit — The Grand Equalizer
**RPV = Conversion Rate x AOV**

You can't control Meta's CPM or CPC. You CAN control conversion rate and AOV. Higher RPV means every click is worth more, directly reducing effective CPP.

If RPV is $3.00 and CPC is $1.50, each click generates $1.50 of gross value. Improve RPV through:
- AOV: bundles, upsells, free shipping threshold
- CR: better product pages, social proof, simplified checkout
- Email: capture more visitors → convert via flows (zero ad cost)

## Scale Decision
- **Only scale when equation is profitable** (Situation 1)
- **NEVER scale in Situation 5** — fix the equation first
- Scale test: increase spend 20-30% for 7-14 days, monitor equation daily
- If ratios hold, lock in new baseline. If not, revert.

### Greenlight to Scale Checkpoints
1. Campaign performance consistently green-light (CPA, frequency, reach)
2. Your own traffic light benchmarks established
3. Frequency under control in 14-day window
4. CPA stable across date range comparisons (14d, 7d, 3-4d)

## Common Issues
- Platform concentration risk (80%+ spend on Meta)
- Summer (Dec-Feb): Lower ROAS as skincare demand drops
- Creative fatigue every 2-3 weeks
- Meta over-reports by ~20-30% vs Shopify actual
- DTC ads may create halo effect driving wholesale (Farmers) sales — true value may be higher than DTC MER suggests
