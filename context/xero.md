# Xero (P&L / Ecommerce Equation) — Noody Skincare Context

## Ecommerce Equation Methodology
The core framework: **Revenue - Variable Costs - Marketing - Fixed Costs = Profit**

Every dollar flows through this equation. The goal is to understand where money is made and lost, then make targeted improvements.

## The Five Situations Framework
1. **Situation 1 — Profitable, Scale It**: Net profit >10%. Equation works. Focus on growing revenue while maintaining ratios.
2. **Situation 2 — High FC, Scale to Spread**: Profitable but constrained. Fixed costs >20% or DTC MER >45%. Revenue growth leverages fixed costs down.
3. **Situation 3 — High VC, Fix Margins**: Variable costs >40%. Fix product margins, negotiate supplier terms, optimize packaging/shipping before scaling.
4. **Situation 4 — High MER, Diagnose CPP vs RPV**: Marketing >35% of revenue. Either CPP is too high (ad problem) or RPV is too low (conversion/AOV problem). Diagnose which.
5. **Situation 5 — Edge Case, Fix MER then VC then Scale**: Unprofitable. Fix in order: marketing efficiency → variable costs → then scale.

## Three Constraints
### Bandwidth
- Stock availability, production capacity, founder time
- Can you fulfill 2x the orders without breaking?
- Wholesale accounts: some growing, some declining — needs stabilization

### Efficiency
- Are you getting good return on every dollar spent?
- Key ratios: MER, ROAS, CPP, email %, variable cost %
- Fixed costs don't mean you're inefficient — they mean you need more revenue

### Throughput
- How much revenue can you push through the business?
- Scale revenue to leverage fixed costs
- At $2M revenue, fixed costs drop from ~25% to ~14%

## Fixed Cost Leverage (The #1 Insight)
Fixed costs are ~$19,840/month regardless of revenue. This means:
- At $946K revenue: FC = 25.2% (above 20% target)
- At $1.5M revenue: FC = ~17% (within target)
- At $2M revenue: FC = ~14% (excellent)
- **Don't cut fixed costs. Grow revenue to leverage them.**

## The Double Revenue Scenario
At $2M revenue with same cost structure + ~$42K additional capacity costs:
- Variable costs remain ~35% (proportional)
- Marketing at 25% target = $500K
- Fixed costs drop to ~14%
- **Profit jumps from $143K to ~$280K+ (14%+ margin)**
