# Xero (P&L / Ecommerce Equation) — Noody Skincare Context

## The Daily Equation
The foundation of everything in the Ecommerce Equation:

**R - MC - VC - FC = $ (Profit)**

- **R** = Revenue (all money in — DTC, wholesale, international)
- **MC** = Marketing Costs (ad spend, agency fees, creative production)
- **VC** = Variable Costs (COGS, shipping, packaging, transaction fees — travel with every order)
- **FC** = Fixed Costs (wages, rent, software, insurance — don't change with sales volume)
- **$** = Profit (what's left)

**The equation must be profitable before you scale.** Scaling an unprofitable equation just makes losses bigger, faster.

## The Five Situations Diagnostic
Every business fits one of five situations. Diagnose first, then act:

### Situation 1 — Profitable, Scale It
- Net profit >10%, ratios in healthy range
- **Action:** Scale revenue. Run Scale Challenges. Increase ad spend 20-30% gradually.
- **Risk:** Scaling too fast can push you into Situation 2 or 4

### Situation 2 — High Fixed Costs, Scale to Spread
- FC >20% of revenue. Profitable at the unit level, not enough volume to leverage FC.
- **Action:** Grow revenue. At $2M revenue, $240K FC = 12%. At $1M, same FC = 24%.
- **Do NOT** cut FC that enables growth (people, systems). Cut genuinely wasteful FC only.

### Situation 3 — High Variable Costs, Fix Margins
- VC >40%. Every order costs too much to fulfill.
- **Action:** Renegotiate suppliers, optimize packaging, increase AOV (spreads VC), review product mix.
- **Do NOT** scale until VC is under control — scaling high VC multiplies the problem.

### Situation 4 — High MER, Diagnose CPP vs RPV
- Marketing >35%. Ads aren't efficient enough.
- **Diagnose:** Is CPP too high (ad problem) or RPV too low (website/offer problem)?
- CPP high → Fix creative, audiences, campaign structure
- RPV low → Fix conversion rate, AOV, email capture, website experience

### Situation 5 — Edge Case, Fix in Order
- Unprofitable. Multiple ratios out of range.
- **Fix in order:** (1) MER → (2) VC → (3) Then scale
- **NEVER scale in Situation 5.**

## The Three Constraints
Every business is limited by one or more:

### Bandwidth (Capacity)
- Stock, production, founder time, team capacity
- Signs: stockouts, shipping delays, burnout, dropping balls
- **Fix:** Hire, outsource, systematize, increase production runs

### Efficiency (Margins & Costs)
- Return on every dollar spent
- Signs: profitable at low volume but margins collapse at scale
- **Fix:** Reduce VC, improve RPV, optimize ad spend, increase email %

### Throughput (Traffic & Conversion)
- How much revenue you push through the business
- Signs: good margins, good efficiency, just not enough volume
- **Fix:** Scale ads, expand geos, launch new channels, increase conversion rate

**Identify which constraint is your bottleneck.** Fixing the wrong one wastes time and money.

## Cost Structure (Variable Costs)
Variable costs travel with EVERY order. They don't go away with scale — must be addressed directly.
- **COGS**: Raw materials, manufacturing, product cost
- **Shipping/Postage**: Courier fees, packaging materials
- **Packaging**: Boxes, labels, inserts
- **Transaction Fees**: Payment processor, Shopify fees
- **Rebates/Returns**: Product returns, refunds

**Contribution Margin** = Revenue - Variable Costs
- DTC: 40-60% target
- Wholesale: 25-40%
- International: 30-50%

## Cost Structure (Fixed Costs)
Fixed costs DON'T change with revenue — this is the leverage:
- **Wages**, **Rent**, **Software**, **Insurance**, **Consultancy**, **General expenses**
- Double revenue with same FC and profit margin jumps dramatically
- **Don't cut FC that enables growth. Grow revenue to make FC a smaller percentage.**

## LER (Labour Efficiency Ratio)
**LER = Gross Margin / Total Labour Costs**
- $2.00+ = Healthy (generating $2+ of gross margin per $1 of labor)
- $1.50-2.00 = Watch
- <$1.50 = Too much labor relative to margin
