# The Scale Challenge - Condensed Framework
Source: Ecommerce Equation (Skool) - The Scale Challenge module

## Overview
The Scale Challenge is a structured approach to systematically push ad spend to find a new profit baseline. Two ways to scale:
1. **BAU Incremental Scale** - day-to-day reactive/proactive adjustments ("scale as you go")
2. **Scale Challenge** - forcing budget into the account over a defined period to discover the true ceiling of the business

The goal is either (a) lock in a higher baseline of revenue and profit, or (b) uncover exactly what is constraining growth.

## The Scale Calculator

### Inputs Required
- **GST rate** - 10% for AU, 15% for NZ, blended if selling internationally
- **Current MER** - what MER is RIGHT NOW, not historical average
- **Variable Cost Ratio (VCR)** - from the ecommerce equation
- **Fixed Cost (daily $)** - all fixed costs divided into a daily figure
- **Current daily revenue** - recent BAU average (be reality-based, not cherry-picking good days)

### How It Works
- Enter current metrics, then model revenue at increasing increments (e.g. $200/day, $500/day, $1000/day steps)
- At each level, the tool shows:
  - Required daily ad spend (at current MER)
  - VCR stays relative (it's a percentage)
  - FCR shrinks as revenue grows (fixed costs spread over more revenue)
  - Resulting profit margin at each level
- **Breakeven point**: the revenue level where profit turns from negative to positive
- **Efficiency loss column**: model what happens if MER increases 5%, 10%, 20% as you scale

### Key Insight: Fixed Cost Leverage
- If FCR is above 20%, there is enormous leverage from scale - FCR drops fast as revenue grows
- The profit points gained from FCR reduction can absorb significant MER blowout
- Example: FCR drops from 32% to 16% when you double the business = 16 points gained, which can absorb a large MER increase

### Red Flags in the Calculator
- If you must 3x-4x revenue just to reach breakeven: scaling is NOT the right move yet
- If you must double the business to reach breakeven with even small efficiency loss: high risk
- If MER is already 40%+ and VCR is 40%+: very little room for efficiency loss

### Green Light Signals
- All rows show green (profitable) even with efficiency loss modeled in
- Breakeven is close to current revenue or already in profit
- MER can rise 20-50% and you're still in a reasonable profit position

## The Greenlight to Scale - 6 Checkpoints

Before committing to a scale challenge, verify ALL six:

### 1. Scale Calculator Says GO
- Profitability holds at target revenue levels even with modeled efficiency loss
- Breakeven is achievable (not requiring 3x+ the business)

### 2. Traffic Mix Check
- Compare **equation cost per visit** (blended: all sessions / total spend) vs **ad account cost per outbound click**
- Three scenarios:
  - **High variance** (e.g. $0.50 equation CPV vs $2.00 ad CPC): lots of organic/email traffic suppressing CPV. Higher risk of efficiency loss when scaling because you're adding expensive paid traffic to a cheap-traffic base
  - **Mid variance**: moderate risk
  - **Low variance** (e.g. $1.50 equation CPV vs $1.80 ad CPC): most traffic already from ads. Lower risk - scaling won't dramatically change your blended metrics
- High variance = expect bigger MER blowout when scaling

### 3. Timing & Environment
- Is the market favorable? (not off-season for your category)
- No major sale hangover suppressing performance
- Good stock levels - don't scale if bestsellers are depleted
- Best scaling windows: Sep-Oct pre-BFCM (ads still relatively cheap, customers acquired now are most valuable cohort for November)
- Avoid scaling in peak-cost periods (late Oct through Nov) when CPMs double

### 4. Campaign Structure Is Optimized
- Campaigns meeting scaling benchmarks for reach, frequency, and new customer acquisition
- Not scaling into a broken or unoptimized account structure

### 5. Creative Volume to Sustain Scale
- Can you produce enough creative to feed higher spend levels?
- At higher spend, creative burns out faster (reaching colder audiences)
- Need fresh creative pipeline before attempting scale
- Rule of thumb: have new creative ready BEFORE you push spend up

### 6. Profit Tolerance / Willingness to Take a Haircut
- Especially relevant pre-BFCM: willing to run at breakeven or thin profit in Sep-Oct to build audiences for November
- Accept that MER will likely spike initially - hold for 1-2 weeks to see if it normalizes
- Worst case: the extra spend is burned. Best case: you double the business

## Scale Test Execution

### Budget Increment Rules
- **Two ways to scale simultaneously:**
  - **Vertically**: increase budget on existing campaigns (stay within ~20% daily increase guideline)
  - **Horizontally**: add new campaigns to increase total daily budget
- Best practice: do BOTH at the same time for faster scaling
- Start with a clear target: e.g. "get from $300/day to $600/day spend" or "double daily revenue"

### Typical Scale Targets by Starting Point
- $300/day spend -> aim for $600-$900/day (2-3x)
- $500/day spend -> aim for $1,000/day
- Small brands (<$1K/day rev): go up in $200-$500/day revenue increments
- Larger brands ($5K+/day rev): go up in $1,000/day revenue increments

### Hold Period
- After pushing spend up, HOLD at the new level for 10 days to 2 weeks
- MER will almost certainly spike initially (8-9 times out of 10)
- Do NOT pull back immediately - let it normalize
- "Set up camp at the new altitude" - get used to conditions before climbing further

### When MER Blows Out During Test
- If MER normalizes within 1-2 weeks: SUCCESS - make this your new baseline
- If MER stays elevated but you're still profitable: evaluate if the volume is worth the margin compression
- If MER stays bad after 2 weeks and you're unprofitable: pull back to previous level, diagnose bottleneck, then try again

## MER / VCR / FCR Scenarios & Responses

### Ready to Scale (Green Light)
**Profile:** MER <30%, VCR <35%, FCR >15% (room to compress)
- Calculator all green, profit holds even with 20-50% MER increase
- Fixed costs drop fast, creating profit buffer
- **Action:** Push spend aggressively. Can scale within days. The real risk is playing too small
- Example: MER 18%, VCR 31%, FCR dropping from 8% to 2% at 4x scale = MER can almost double and you're still ahead

### Bumpy MER
**Profile:** MER volatile day-to-day, equation borderline profitable
- Common at smaller revenue bases where daily swings are more pronounced
- **Action:** Scale during stable profitable period with good stock. Hold 1-2 weeks at new level. If MER normalizes at new altitude, stay. If not, pull back and work on AOV/CR/RPV

### MER Too High (>40%)
**Profile:** MER 40%+, variable/fluctuating, breakeven requires doubling+ the business
- **Action:** Do NOT scale. Focus on bottleneck diagnosis:
  - Is it cost per visit (marketing problem) or revenue per visit (website/offer problem)?
  - Work on conversion rate, AOV, revenue per visit
  - Get MER under control before attempting scale

### FCR Too High (#1 - Heavy Fixed Costs, Moderate Revenue)
**Profile:** FCR 40-60%, MER reasonable, but breakeven requires near-doubling revenue
- Fixed costs appropriate for a much larger business (e.g. $80K/month FC for a $150K/month brand)
- **Action:** Test small spend increases. If MER doesn't blow out, keep going toward breakeven. Focus hard on AOV and RPV. May need to question pricing and cost structure
- Consider: is the product priced correctly? Are production costs too high?

### FCR Too High (#2 - Must Scale to Survive)
**Profile:** FCR 50%+, MER efficient (e.g. 25%), but business is underwater at current scale
- Fixed costs are crushing margins; standing still is losing money daily
- **Action:** Push to scale URGENTLY - you're spending the money on losses anyway, better to spend it on ads. Even if MER doubles, you'd be in the same position but with more customers. Expand geos (AU + US with 3PL). Get to breakeven as first milestone

### High MER & VCR with Low FCR
**Profile:** MER 40%, VCR 47%, FCR tiny ($33/day)
- Fixed costs already minimal - no leverage from scale to improve margins
- Scaling won't unlock efficiency; all costs are variable
- **Action:** Do NOT scale yet. Focus entirely on AOV: bundles, GWPs, pricing. Consider international expansion (remove GST, price higher). Shipping cost as % of AOV is the diagnostic - if >15%, that's a red flag. Get VCR down first, then scale

### High MER & VCR with Mid FCR
**Profile:** MER 43%, VCR 40%, FCR ~15%
- Some fixed cost leverage available but limited room for MER blowout
- **Action:** Test a push - if MER holds, fixed costs drop and profit lifts. If MER blows out, pull back and work on AOV/RPV/VCR. Long-term play: optimize cost structure now so when good efficiency periods come, you capture more profit

### VCR Too High
**Profile:** VCR 55%, MER historically low (8-12%) but rising as spend increases
- At mercy of variable costs - can't let MER blow out much
- **Action:** Test incremental spend increases. If MER holds at 12%, scale up carefully. If it rises further, breakeven moves fast. Double down on AOV (bundles, GWP, packaging optimization to reduce shipping cost). Getting AOV up is the single biggest lever

## Creative Testing for Scale

### Hero vs Sidekick Ads
- **Hero ads**: high reach, high engagement, may have higher CPA but feed the funnel and help other ads convert. Don't turn off just because CPA looks expensive in isolation
- **Sidekick ads**: support heroes, may convert cheaper but with less reach
- Judge on blended MER, not individual ad CPA

### Testing Structure
- Test in **batches** (batch 1, batch 2, etc.) within ad sets
- Keep category consistent within each batch (e.g. all jeans together)
- Name batches clearly for tracking

### What to Look For in Winners
- Ads that "suck the budget" - Meta allocates more spend to them naturally
- Acceptable CPA relative to the campaign type
- Sustained lower frequency for a longer period (signal of broad appeal)
- Good reach-to-frequency ratio

### Testing Budget Guidelines
- ABO testing: $50-$100/day per ad set
- CBO testing: start with 2-3 ad sets, $200+ campaign budget, set $30 minimum spend per ad set
- Cost Cap testing: $100-$500/day per ad set (non-SIBS), $10K/ad set for SIBS
- LPV testing (landing page views): $10-$20/day per ad set (cheap testing for high AOV brands)

### The 4 Campaign Scenarios for Creative Testing
1. **Testing goes well, scaling goes well** - best case, keep scaling the winners
2. **Testing goes well, scaling doesn't work** - creative is engaging but not converting at scale; work on website/offer
3. **Testing doesn't work, scaling goes well** - existing creative is strong enough; focus on fresh testing pipeline
4. **Testing doesn't work, scaling doesn't work** - back to fundamentals; creative AND conversion need work

## Campaign Structures for Scaling

### ABO - Broad (With/No Exclusions)
**Best for:** Experienced advertisers who want granular control
- Budget set at ad set level
- Pros: full control per ad set, new ad sets don't disrupt existing ones, flexible categorization
- Cons: heavy monitoring required, risk of overspending on losers, total budget can creep up across many ad sets
- Watch out: don't let testing campaign become your highest-spend campaign

### CBO - Broad (With/No Exclusions)
**Best for:** Busy founders with less time to optimize
- Budget set at campaign level, Meta auto-allocates to winning ad sets
- Set **minimum spend per ad set** ($30+) to ensure all batches get data
- Pros: less optimization needed, winners identified automatically
- Cons: new ad sets steal budget from existing ones, less control, some ad sets may get minimal spend
- Start with 2-3 ad sets

### Cost Cap ABO - Broad (With/No Exclusions)
**Best for:** High creative volume, experienced advertisers
- Set cost-per-result goal based on **new audience CPA** (not blended CPA)
- Budget only spent if target CPA can be hit (in theory)
- Two approaches:
  - **Conservative (non-SIBS):** $100-$500 per ad set budget, for brands spending <$2K/day
  - **SIBS (Cap Inflated Budget Strategy):** $10K+ per ad set with cost cap. Tells Meta to bid aggressively in auctions for quality placements. Only for brands spending $2K+/day
- Must set **campaign spending limit** as safety net (start at $2K-$5K)
- If ad sets get no spend: increase cost-per-result goal by $2-$5 increments
- Ad set spending limits: use daily maximum to prevent overspend (CBO version only)

### MOF-ABO-All Mid (Mid-Funnel Testing)
**Best for:** Brands with large mid-funnel audiences
- Cheaper to test, performance looks good (warm audience is forgiving)
- Good for testing new products/categories and pushing slow sellers
- Cons: winners may not translate to TOF, frequency burns out fast, must move winners into TOF to scale
- Keep budgets lower than TOF campaigns
- Adjust benchmarks: mid-funnel CPA will be cheaper, frequency tolerance is lower (2x frequency at 20K reach is fine for MOF, terrible for TOF)

### TOF-ABO-Sales-LPV-Super Harsh (Landing Page Views)
**Best for:** Small budgets or high AOV brands ($300+ AOV)
- Sales campaign optimized for landing page views (not purchases)
- Super harsh exclusions: exclude email list, followers, all purchasers, all video views, all engagement
- $10-$20/day per ad set (very cheap testing)
- Look for: which ads suck the budget, best CTR relative to each other
- Cons: metrics don't compare to purchase campaigns, may skew site-wide CR/RPV
- Keep budget small to avoid polluting conversion data

### TOM-Old ASC (10-20% Customer Cap)
**Best for:** Brands where old ASC still outperforms
- Covers colder section of mid-funnel (top-of-mid)
- What works here commonly works in cold TOF
- Cons: customer caps non-adjustable, only 1 ad set at a time, can't create new ones (Meta sunsetting), risk of spending on engaged audience
- Use while it lasts but don't depend on it long-term

### SIBS (Cap Inflated Budget Strategy)
**Best for:** Brands spending $2K+/day with high creative volume
- Set cost cap + intentionally high budget ($10K+) = win more auctions
- Spend flexes with opportunity (may spend $200 one day, $2K the next)
- MUST use safeguards: campaign spending limit + ad set spending limit
- Set cost-per-result goal at new audience CPA level
- Higher risk / higher reward strategy - not for beginners
- Start campaign spending limit at $2K-$5K, increase as you validate performance

## When the Scale Test Fails

### Immediate Response
1. Don't panic after 2-3 bad days - hold for full 10-14 day test period
2. If still bad after 2 weeks: pull back to previous baseline

### Diagnosis Framework (3 Rounds)

**Round 1: Audit & Retry**
- **Campaign structure**: Did campaigns meet scaling benchmarks for reach, frequency, new customers?
- **Creative**: Is creative appealing enough for cold/stranger audiences?
- **Conversion**: Is the website creating friction? Is the offer irresistible enough?
- **Stock levels**: Did bestsellers run out during the scale period?
- Fix identified issues, then run scale test again

**Round 2: Expand & Retry**
- Repeat audit from Round 1
- **Find new niches**: Who else could use your product? New angles, new use cases
- **Expand geos**: Sell into new countries (International Expansion)
- Fix and run scale test again

**Round 3: Diversify & Retry**
- Repeat audit from Round 1
- **New channel**: Where else is your niche spending time? (Google, TikTok, etc.)
- **Expand product range**: Complementary products to hero SKUs, or entirely new hero products
- Fix and run scale test again

### Root Cause Categories
- **Traffic problem (high cost per visit)**: Ads issue - creative fatigue, wrong audiences, poor campaign structure
- **Conversion problem (low revenue per visit)**: Website/offer issue - low CR, low AOV, poor UX
- **Both**: Most common. Work on both simultaneously

## Scale Challenge Review - What "Working" Looks Like

### Success Metrics (14-day test)
- Daily revenue at or above target level consistently
- MER stabilized (not necessarily at pre-scale level, but stable)
- Profitability in acceptable range (even if slightly compressed)
- New customer acquisition rate healthy
- Stock levels maintained

### Winning Result
- Revenue baseline lifted, profit maintained or only slightly compressed
- **Next step**: Make this your new normal. Work on AOV/CR/RPV to reclaim profit points lost to higher MER

### Broke Even Result
- Revenue higher but profit flat or zero
- Still valuable: you now know the ceiling at current efficiency
- **Next step**: Optimize website, offers, AOV to convert the extra traffic more profitably. Then try again

### Losing Result
- MER blew out, couldn't sustain higher revenue profitably
- Still valuable: you now know where the bottleneck is
- **Next step**: Follow the 3-round diagnosis framework above

## Key Benchmarks & Rules of Thumb

### Equation Benchmarks
- **MER**: <30% good, 30-40% acceptable, >40% needs work before scaling
- **VCR**: <35% good, 35-45% acceptable, >45% needs work (AOV/pricing/shipping)
- **FCR**: 10-20% is efficient range. >20% means high leverage from scale. <10% means limited leverage (all costs variable)
- **Net profit**: 10-30% is normal range. 10% is typical, 30% is exceptional

### Scaling Rules
- MER will blow out 8-9 times out of 10 when you scale
- Hold new spend level for 10-14 days before deciding
- Fixed costs shrink by ~half when you double the business
- Worst case of scaling = you burn the incremental spend difference. Best case = you double the business
- Each new "altitude" of spend has different demands: creative cadence, optimization approach, demand planning speed
- It often takes 3-5 attempts to successfully scale to the next level
- Customers acquired in Sep-Oct are the most valuable cohort (they come back for BFCM)

### Campaign Benchmarks
- ABO testing budget: $50-$100/day per ad set
- CBO minimum spend per ad set: $30/day
- Cost Cap SIBS: only for brands spending $2K+/day
- LPV testing: $10-$20/day (don't overspend or it pollutes conversion data)
- Mid-funnel frequency >3.4x: time to move winners to TOF
- Shipping cost >15% of AOV: red flag, needs attention

### Scale Speed Guidelines
- Can scale from current to 2x spend within 2-3 days if equation supports it
- Can potentially 3x spend within a week for very efficient equations
- More conservative: double spend over 1-2 weeks
- After each push, stabilize for 1-2 weeks before pushing again
