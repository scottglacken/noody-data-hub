## 1. THE CREATIVE-FIRST PARADIGM

### Why Creative Is the New Targeting
- Post iOS 14, Meta's AI replaced interest-based targeting with intent-based delivery
- Advantage Plus Shopping campaigns use AI to find converters independent of interest categories
- Creative is no longer a piece of media buying -- it IS the strategic edge
- The performance pyramid has flipped: Creative > Data Analysis > Media Buying
- "All of the pricing power has gone to creative" (Jeff, Meta Head of Agencies NA)
- Top US creative agencies (Tube Science, Donut Digital, Narrative Digital) have zero media buyers but hundreds of creative strategists and creators
- Tube Science: 40 clients, $1.8B in Meta spend, avg $3M/month per client -- all creative-led

### The Shift Timeline
- Pre-2021: Stable interest-based targeting, media buying was the edge
- 2021: iOS 14 killed data flow from Apple apps to Meta, targeting went "drunk"
- 2022-23: Advantage Plus launched, AI-driven targeting restored performance
- 2023-24: Meta pivoted $10B/yr metaverse investment into AI infrastructure (GPUs repurposed)
- 2024: Reels became dominant ad placement, organic-style content outperformed polished ads
- 2025-26: Creative-first world -- creative diversity and volume are the primary performance levers

### Key Principle: Create Like a Child, Edit Like a Scientist
- Ideation phase: experiment freely, have fun, be raw and authentic
- Optimization phase: use data, iterate methodically, be scientific about what works

## 2. THE HERO CUSTOMER FRAMEWORK

### Four Types of "Hero" in Ads
1. **Product as Hero**: "This is an amazing product" -- shows features, specs
2. **Brand as Hero**: "We've been around since 1929" -- company story, heritage
3. **Result as Hero**: "We'll help you stabilize your knee" -- functional outcome
4. **Customer as Hero**: "I went from daily knee pain to 20 points per game" -- customer's journey and transformation

### Customer as Hero = Top of Funnel Winner
- Customer-as-hero ads are phenomenal at finding cold audiences
- Product/brand/result ads work better for middle and bottom of funnel
- The customer's quest, plight, and desires should be the focus, not features
- Example: A teen basketball player doesn't want "knee stabilization" -- he wants to play a full game and score 20 points

### The Three Brains (Decision-Making Triggers)
Every purchase decision activates one of three brains:
1. **Head (Strategy/Logic)**: Rational, analytical -- "Am I being smart? Do the numbers make sense?"
2. **Heart (Emotion)**: Feelings, connection -- "How does this make me feel? Do I trust this?"
3. **Gut (Instinct)**: Impulse, identity -- "This is me. I need this now."
- Effective hooks tap into one of these three brains in the first 0-3 seconds
- Different customers respond to different brains -- diversify your approach

### Hero Customer Mapping Process
1. Use Product Market Fit Finder to discover customer segments (9 at a time)
2. Feed segments into Hero Customer Simulator to understand goals, motivations, buying decisions
3. Build 5-9 customer snapshot profiles (blue sheet)
4. Map each profile's fears, frustrations, wants, aspirations
5. Generate infinite ad snapshots (red sheet) from each profile

## 3. THE 12 CONVERSATIONS FRAMEWORK

### Core Principle
- No one NEEDS your product -- your job is to make it a WANT and DESIRE
- "Why should they choose us?" is the starting question for unlimited creative
- One interesting thing at a time, in different formats = hundreds of pieces of creative
- Two big mistakes: (1) Product with no context, (2) Playing the same note

### The Four Creative Pillars
Each pillar creates a different perception. All four must be balanced:

#### Pillar 1: SHARE EDUCATION
- You as the expert teaching what you know that customers don't
- Makes your product perceived as VALUABLE
- Closes the gap between price and perceived worth
- Risk if overused: becomes BORING
- Conversations: Reasons Why, Behind the Scenes, How It Works

#### Pillar 2: SHOW PROOF
- Customers and users validating the product (not you)
- Makes customers feel SAFE -- reduces the "zone of doubt" after purchase
- Risk if overused: comes across as PRETENTIOUS
- Conversations: Customer Testimonials, Reviews, User-Generated Content

#### Pillar 3: CONNECT TO FEARS & DESIRES
- Speak to what customers fear and what they aspire to
- Makes content feel INSPIRING -- people want to live into that future
- Can be spoken, unspoken, or unknown fears/desires
- Risk if overused: comes across as FAKE
- Conversations: Problem-Solution, Aspiration, Transformation

#### Pillar 4: SPEAK YOUR OPINION
- Share your unique perspective, values, stance
- Makes your brand feel AUTHENTIC and creates massive differentiation
- "If you don't have an opinion, you're just a hammock"
- People who agree will love you; people who don't weren't going to buy anyway
- Risk if overused: comes across as PREACHY
- Conversations: Us vs Them, My Picks & Trends, Industry Takes

### The Balance
- Education + Proof + Fears/Desires + Opinion = Valuable + Safe + Inspiring + Authentic
- Neglecting any one = risk of being Boring, Pretentious, Fake, or Preachy
- Diversify across all four to reach new audiences and keep content fresh

### 12 Specific Conversation Types (3 per pillar)
**Education**: Reasons Why | Behind the Curtain | How-To/Education
**Proof**: Customer Review/Testimonial | Customer Using Product | Social Proof (numbers)
**Fears & Desires**: Problem-Solution | Aspiration/Transformation | Fear-Based Hook
**Opinion**: Us vs Them | My Picks & Trends | Bold Statement/Industry Take

## 4. LAYERS OF DISTINCTION (Funnel-Stage Creative)

### Top of Funnel (Strangers)
- Go raw, authentic, engaging, entertaining
- UGC-style, native-feeling content
- Focus on customer-as-hero
- Pattern interrupt hooks
- Don't worry about brand aesthetic here

### Middle of Funnel (Warm Audiences)
- Start layering in brand elements
- Show proof, social validation
- Education-heavy content
- Product demonstrations with context
- Retarget with complementary messages

### Bottom of Funnel (Hot Audiences)
- Strong brand aesthetic acceptable
- Direct offers, urgency, scarcity
- Result-focused content
- Reviews and testimonials
- Clear CTAs

### Key Insight
- "Get a little crazy at top of funnel, bring it back at middle and bottom"
- Continue polished brand content for MOF/BOF, but TOF needs to be different
- Brand that added 30% revenue by trying a different creative approach for US expansion

## 5. HOOK FORMULAS & SCRIPTING

### Hook Fundamentals
- First 0-3 seconds are critical -- must stop the scroll
- Build curiosity while providing context
- Tap into one of the three brains (head, heart, gut)

### Hook Categories

#### Gossip Hooks
- Make audience feel like they're getting an inside scoop
- "I probably shouldn't be telling you this, but..."
- Shows up natively, feels like friend sharing a secret
- Great for cut-through in crowded feeds

#### Bold Statement Hooks
- Lead with a provocative or unexpected claim
- Pattern interrupt that stops scrolling
- Must be backed up with substance in the body

#### Problem-Solution Hooks
- Open with a relatable problem/frustration
- "Tired of [common frustration]?"
- Immediately pivot to the solution
- Works across all product categories

#### Myth & Friction Hooks
- Challenge common beliefs or misconceptions
- "Everyone thinks [myth] but actually..."
- Creates cognitive friction that demands attention
- Great for education-pillar content

#### Fear-Based Hooks
- Tap into unspoken concerns
- "What no one tells you about..."
- Must be handled authentically, not manipulatively

### Scripting Frameworks

#### The 4-Reason Frame
- Hook + 4 specific reasons why + CTA
- Each reason is a mini-argument
- Works for education and opinion pillars
- Easy to iterate by swapping reasons

#### Problem-Solution Script
1. Identify the problem (be specific and relatable)
2. Agitate the problem (show consequences)
3. Introduce the solution (your product/brand)
4. Show the transformation (customer-as-hero result)
5. CTA

#### Personify Your Product Script
- Give the product a personality or character
- Collaborate with talent/influencers who embody the product
- The product becomes part of the person's identity story

## 6. CREATIVE PRODUCTION & TESTING

### The Creative Catalyst Process (3 Steps)
1. **Ideate**: Research who you're selling to, what's happening in market, mine reviews/comments/social listening
2. **Capture**: Map out creative concepts using customer snapshots, plan formats and styles, shoot/film content
3. **Shortlist**: Step back, evaluate against data, identify likely winners, test methodically

### Content Volume & Frequency
- Post organically daily (minimum) -- ideally 1-3x per day
- Quality matters more than volume, but volume matters once quality is consistent
- TikTok is a great testing ground -- "Be dirty. Have fun. No one knows. No one cares."
- Cross-post winning organic content as paid ads
- Organic posting is free practice for paid creative

### Creative Diversity
- Don't play the same note -- diversify across pillars, formats, and audiences
- Same creator, different approach = new creative
- Same message, different format = new creative
- Iterate winners by changing: scene, shot style, narrator, hook, setting
- Use the Creative Mixmaster GPT to generate iteration ideas

### Iterating Winners
- When you find a winning ad, don't just leave it running
- Iterate the hook (same body, different opening)
- Iterate the visual style (same script, different setting/talent)
- Iterate the format (video to image carousel, or vice versa)
- Iterate the audience angle (same product, different hero customer)
- Repurpose sale content for BAU by removing time-sensitive elements

### Whitelisting (Partnership Ads)
- Run ads from creator/influencer pages instead of brand page
- Ad appears as personal recommendation, not brand advertisement
- Can create niche-specific whitelisted pages (e.g., "Fashionista's Best Picks")
- Seeing strong results across multiple brands
- Great way to tap into new audience segments

## 7. UGC STRATEGY & BEST PRACTICES

### Why UGC Works
- Feels native to the platform (doesn't look like an ad)
- Triggers trust through social proof
- Cheaper to produce than polished brand content
- Performs exceptionally well at top of funnel
- Meta's algorithm favors authentic, engaging content

### Founder Content
- Founder-led content consistently cited as top performer across brands
- During BFCM, "founder" was the most common winning creative style
- Founder hype content, founder product demonstrations, founder opinion pieces
- Don't need to be polished -- authenticity wins
- Personal brand content framework: show up within business, alongside business, or with team

### Creator/Influencer Content
- Brief creators with specific pillar + conversation type
- Use the Affiliate Content Creation Guide GPT for brand-aligned briefs
- Don't try to control every word -- let creators be natural
- Film multiple angles/concepts in one session
- Repurpose across organic and paid

### Content Formats That Work
- Behind-the-scenes (education pillar)
- Try-on/unboxing with real reactions (proof pillar)
- "Day in my life" featuring product naturally (desire pillar)
- Opinion pieces / hot takes (opinion pillar)
- Reply-to-comment format (engagement + proof)
- Transition videos and trend participation
- Memes and humor (for organic reach)

## 8. VIRAL CONTENT CHECKLIST

### Viral by Design (Not by Accident)
- Going viral follows a framework -- it is not random
- Elements that drive virality:
  - **Relatability**: Content that makes people say "that's so me"
  - **Emotion**: Makes people laugh, cry, feel inspired, or get angry
  - **Shareability**: People want to tag a friend or share to stories
  - **Pattern interrupt**: Something unexpected in the first 1-2 seconds
  - **Trend participation**: Lean into current conversations, memes, sounds
  - **Native format**: Looks like organic content, not a polished ad

### Virality Multipliers
- Music and trending audio
- Text overlay for silent viewers
- Quick cuts and dynamic editing
- Engaging captions that drive comments
- Strategic use of hooks that build curiosity
- Lean into cultural moments and seasonality

## 9. SALE & SEASONAL CREATIVE

### Sale Creative Phases
1. **Hype Phase**: Build anticipation, FOMO, excitement, readiness
   - Use emotional levers: excitement, scarcity hints, countdown
   - Founder hype content performs very well
2. **Launch Phase**: Announce the offer clearly, drive urgency
   - Bold statements, clear value proposition
   - Multiple creative angles for the same offer
3. **Mid-Sale Phase**: Combat fatigue, re-engage, add new angles
   - New creative hooks for the same offer
   - Social proof from early buyers
   - Scarcity messaging (limited stock, time running out)
4. **End Phase**: Final push urgency, last chance messaging
   - Countdown timers, "closing tonight" language
   - Testimonials from satisfied sale buyers
5. **Post-Sale**: Pivot back to BAU content
   - Iterate sale content by removing time-sensitive elements
   - Gifting angles for seasonal transition

### Gifting Creative
- Position any product as a gift during holiday season
- "Gift for the person who..." framing
- Gift guides, curated sets, bundles
- Works from mid-November through February (Valentine's Day)

### Urgency Drivers (Beyond Scarcity)
- Social proof urgency ("50,000 people already love this")
- Seasonal relevance ("perfect for summer")
- Problem urgency ("don't go another day with...")
- Transformation urgency ("start the year as...")
- FOMO through community ("join the movement")

## 12. KEY METRICS & BENCHMARKS

### Creative Performance Signals
- **Hook rate**: % of viewers who watch past 3 seconds -- primary indicator of hook effectiveness
- **Hold rate / ThruPlay**: % watching to completion -- indicates content quality
- **CTR (Click-Through Rate)**: Measures curiosity and intent generation
- **CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)**: Ultimate measure of creative effectiveness
- **CPM (Cost Per Thousand)**: Lower when creative is engaging (algorithm rewards good content)

### Creative Fatigue Signals
- Rising CPA with stable or increasing spend
- Declining CTR over time on same creative
- Frequency climbing above 2-3 for cold audiences
- Declining hook rate on previously strong creative
- Solution: Iterate the creative (new hook, new angle, new format) rather than killing it

### Production Volume Benchmarks
- Minimum: 3-5 new creative concepts per week for testing
- Ideal: New creative going into ad account weekly
- Organic: 1-3 posts per day across platforms
- Iteration: 3-5 variations of each winning concept
- Creative testing should be continuous, not campaign-based

### What's Working (2025-2026 Market Data)
- Founder content: consistently top performer across brands
- UGC/authentic style: outperforming polished brand content at TOF
- Short-form video (Reels/TikTok): dominant format
- Reply-to-comment format: high engagement
- Problem-solution scripts: reliable conversion driver
- Opinion/hot-take content: strong for engagement and brand building
- Transition videos and trend participation: good for organic reach

## 14. CREATIVE STRATEGY CHECKLIST

### Weekly Creative Actions
- [ ] Review winning ads -- what pillar, what hook, what format?
- [ ] Ideate 3-5 new concepts across different pillars
- [ ] Film/create minimum 3 new pieces of content
- [ ] Post organically daily (Reels, TikTok, Stories)
- [ ] Iterate 1-2 winning ads with new hooks/angles

### Monthly Creative Actions
- [ ] Audit creative mix: are all 4 pillars represented?
- [ ] Refresh hero customer profiles -- any new segments to target?
- [ ] Review seasonal/calendar opportunities for next 4-6 weeks
- [ ] Test one completely new format or style
- [ ] Run GPT suite for fresh idea generation
- [ ] Evaluate whitelisting/partnership opportunities

### Creative Diversity Check
- Am I speaking to more than one customer segment?
- Am I using more than one creative format (video, image, carousel)?
- Am I tapping into all four pillars (education, proof, fears/desires, opinion)?
- Am I testing different hooks for the same message?
- Am I iterating winners, not just launching new concepts?
- Am I showing up on multiple platforms (Meta, TikTok, organic)?

## 15. THE THREE TRUTHS MODEL (Deep Customer Insight)

- **Spoken Truths**: Things customers already say/know ("my child has sensitive skin") -- low impact, they already know this
- **Unspoken Truths**: Things they think but haven't told anyone ("I feel guilty when my child scratches") -- high impact, makes them feel heard
- **Unknown Truths**: Things they don't know about themselves yet ("you're not treating eczema, you're managing their comfort") -- highest impact, positions you as genius

The magic is in unspoken and unknown truths. When you articulate someone's situation better than they can, they instantly respect you as authority ("You Get Me" Framework).

## 16. SIX STAGES OF CREATIVE MANAGEMENT

Most brands only do stages 1-4 and skip 5-6 (where the magic happens):

1. **Ideate** — Research, blue sheets, brainstorm
2. **Shoot** — Capture content
3. **Edit** — Post-production
4. **Publish** — Launch as organic or paid
5. **Evaluate** — Analyze performance (MOST BRANDS SKIP THIS)
6. **Iterate** — Iterate winners with new hooks/faces/formats (MOST BRANDS SKIP THIS)

Bus Factor Test: If you vanished tomorrow, could someone continue where you left off? Track creative through all 6 stages in a project management tool.

## 17. CONVERSION FORMULA

**Conversion = Value + Emotion + Connection**

"Marry Buy with Why" — Never just say buy. Frame the WHY so the buy is effortless.

### Roller Coaster of Promotion Emotions
Use these emotional levers in sale/launch creative:
Readiness → Exclusivity → Anxiety → FOMO → Curiosity → Anticipation → Excitement

### Same Product, Different Problem
Scaling method: find new use cases and audiences for existing products without changing the product itself. Each new problem = new creative = new audience segment the algorithm can find.
