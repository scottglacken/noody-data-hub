# The Promotional Rhythm - Condensed Knowledge Base

> Source: Ecommerce Equation - The Promotional Rhythm module (~24 training files)
> Covers: Annual marketing calendar, promotional cadence, product drops, event planning

---

## 1. CORE PHILOSOPHY

The promotional rhythm is about creating **predictable growth** by engineering a year-round
calendar of promotional activity. The goal is to steadily raise your **revenue and profit average**
rather than obsessing over individual dips. Every brand has natural highs and lows from seasonality -
the objective is to keep pushing the average up.

The framework uses four levers to build a promotional schedule:
1. **Revenue Peaks** - Pre-planned major sale events
2. **Awareness with New** - Product drops and launches
3. **Relevance with Events** - Seasonal/cultural tie-ins
4. **Liquidation with Promos** - Strategic stock clearance

If you do the first three well, you need less of the fourth.

## 2. THE FOUR PROMOTIONAL LEVERS

### Lever 1: Revenue Peaks (3 Per Year)

Plan three deliberate revenue peaks each year to avoid panic discounts and create predictable,
profitable moments. These are your "7 Figure Sale" events.

**Recommended calendar (AU/NZ):**
- **Peak 1: March Sale** (March) - Brand birthday, autumn sale, or stock clearance
  - Shortest and lightest of the three (5-7 days)
  - Lower discount intensity
  - Good for post-summer clearance
  - Helps smooth revenue between January trough and June
- **Peak 2: EOFY Sale** (June) - End of Financial Year
  - Second biggest event
  - 7-10 days typical
  - Clear winter/seasonal stock
  - Can nearly match BFCM for strong brands
- **Peak 3: BFCM** (November) - Black Friday Cyber Monday
  - The biggest event of the year
  - 7-14 days typical
  - 75% of EE members have biggest month ever
  - Has consumed Boxing Day demand entirely

Each peak follows the same framework:
- Hype phase (build anticipation)
- Launch (big opening day)
- Mid-sale offers (maintain momentum)
- Close (urgency push)
- Post-sale recovery

### Lever 2: Awareness with New Products

New arrivals are the ultimate post-sale hangover cure and the most powerful way to maintain
momentum year-round.

**Key principle: Shift from large seasonal collections to smaller, frequent drops.**

Benefits of frequent smaller drops:
- More "reasons to talk" to your audience throughout the year
- Clearer signal on which products perform (vs burying them in big collections)
- More content moments for social, email, and ads
- Keeps the brand feeling fresh and alive
- Reduces risk of big collection flops

**Drop cadence options:**
- Weekly drops (e.g., Tuesday/Thursday at set time - I Love Ugly did 120/year)
- Fortnightly drops
- Monthly drops
- Even "drops" of existing products: bundles, curated edits, "back in stock" moments

**Not every drop needs new product development:**
- Bundle existing products in new ways
- Highlight underperforming products with new creative/positioning
- "Staff picks" or "founder favorites" curations
- Seasonal restocks of bestsellers

### Lever 3: Relevance with Events

Link products to gifting, seasonal, or cultural moments to increase relevance and create
natural promotional opportunities without discounting.

**Key events calendar (AU/NZ context):**
- January: New Year, Back to School
- February: Valentine's Day, Lunar New Year
- March: International Women's Day, Harmony Day, brand birthday sales
- April: Easter, Anzac Day, Earth Day
- May: Mother's Day (major gifting event)
- June: EOFY Sale, Queen's Birthday
- July: Mid-year clearance, NAIDOC Week
- August: Father's Day (AU - first Sunday Sept, so prep in Aug)
- September: Father's Day, Spring Racing
- October: Halloween, Mental Health Week, BFCM prep begins
- November: BFCM, Movember
- December: Christmas (gifting peak), Summer launch

**How to do events well (not just lip service):**
- Create event-specific product bundles or gift packs
- Design occasion-specific creative and landing pages
- Offer gift wrapping, gift cards, or curated "gift guides"
- Plan 2-3 months ahead - not last minute
- The brand must genuinely connect to the occasion (don't force irrelevant tie-ins)

**Gifting is a major opportunity:**
- Gift guides segmented by recipient, price, or occasion
- Gift wrapping or packaging upgrades
- Gift card campaigns for "hard to buy for" messaging
- Express/guaranteed shipping deadlines create natural urgency

### Lever 4: Liquidation with Promos

Strategic clearance of B and C grade stock throughout the year. If you execute the other
three levers well, you need fewer liquidation promos.

**When to use:**
- Demand plan shows week's cover getting out of control
- Seasonal items not moving post-season
- Excess sizes, colors, or discontinued lines
- Cash tied up in dead stock that should be redeployed to winners

**Approaches (that protect brand perception):**
- Hidden "outlet" or "last chance" section on website (not homepage)
- Email-only flash sales to existing customers
- Bundle slow movers with A-grade products
- "Mystery box" or grab bags at deep discount
- Progressive markdowns (start moderate, go deeper over time)
- Use sale events (BFCM, EOFY) as the primary liquidation vehicle

**Key principle:** Recover capital and reinvest in winners. Holding out for full price on dead
stock is a slow cash drain. Better to accept the loss and redeploy into A-grade.

## 3. THE ULTIMATE MARKETING CALENDAR

A 12-month planning tool that maps all four levers onto a single view.

### How to fill in your calendar:

**Step 1: Lock in your 3 Revenue Peaks**
- March sale, EOFY (June), BFCM (November)
- Mark hype start dates, launch dates, end dates
- Block out 3-4 weeks per peak (including hype + post-sale)

**Step 2: Map New Product Drops**
- Decide on frequency (weekly, fortnightly, monthly)
- Plot product development timelines backwards from drop dates
- Identify which drops are "big" (new product development) vs "small" (restocks, bundles)
- Avoid launching new products in the 2 weeks before or during a sale event

**Step 3: Add Event/Seasonal Tie-ins**
- Plot all relevant gifting occasions
- Identify which events your brand can genuinely connect to
- Plan creative assets and offers 2-3 months ahead
- Minimum: Mother's Day, Father's Day, Valentine's Day, Christmas (if gifting relevant)

**Step 4: Schedule Liquidation Windows**
- Identify seasonal stock clearance windows
- Plan liquidation promos to hit before next sale event
- Use data from demand planning (week's cover reports)

**Step 5: Identify "Quiet Zones"**
- January (post-Christmas recovery)
- August (typically quietest month for many AU brands)
- These are content-building, audience-growing, creative-production windows
- Not dead time - invest in TOF audience building for upcoming peak events

## 4. THE NEW PRODUCT DROP SYSTEM

### Pre-Launch (5 days out for major drops)

**Day -5:** Social pushes + email with sneak peek (what's coming, when)
**Day -3:** Giveaway or competition (builds engagement and anticipation)
**Day -2:** Detailed sneak peek with specifics (product shots, pricing hints)
**Day -1:** Final hype push - "Tomorrow at [TIME]" + countdown
**Launch Day:** Execute the 20-minute launch window

For smaller drops (new colorways, restocks), skip the 5-day cadence.
Just release with an email and social post on launch day.

### The 20-Minute Launch Window

Set a consistent day and time (e.g., Tuesday/Thursday 10 AM):
1. **T-20 min:** Final social story teaser
2. **T-0:** Product goes live on website
3. **T+0:** Email blast fires (already scheduled)
4. **T+5-10 min:** SMS blast fires
5. **T+10:** Meta ads with launch creative go live
6. **T+20:** Monitor initial response, adjust if needed

Training your audience to expect releases at a set time builds habitual purchasing behavior.

### Mid-Sale Drops

When running a sale event (BFCM, EOFY), new product drops during the sale create
mid-sale revenue spikes:
- Launch new arrivals on days 4-8 of the sale
- Release one new product/collection per day for 5 consecutive days
- Each day gets a 24-hour only incentive (extra discount, free gift, exclusive access)
- Purpose: re-engage existing customers for second or third purchase during sale

### Post-Sale Drops

Immediately after a sale event, launch something new to minimize the "sale hangover":
- Limited edition, collab, or standout new product
- Does NOT need heavy hype - light touch promotion
- Use existing Advantage+ campaigns, just refresh creative
- Product should feel genuinely different from what was on sale
- Keeps repeat customers buying without requiring 100% TOF spend

## 5. MANAGING REVENUE DIPS

### The Post-Sale Drop in Revenue

After every sale event, revenue will drop below BAU. This is normal and expected.
Key strategies to minimize the dip:

1. **Post-sale product drop** (see above)
2. **Free shipping or express shipping upgrade** leading into gifting periods
3. **New customer nurture** - BFCM brings a flood of new customers who need nurture sequences
4. **Slowly rebuild BAU ad campaigns** (which were paused/reduced during sale)
5. **Do NOT panic discount** - this trains customers to wait for sales
6. **Content and community focus** during recovery periods

### The Mid-Sale Dip in Revenue

Revenue naturally drops after the big launch day. Every day of a sale beyond day 1
needs a fresh reason for customers to engage:
- New offers each day (the Offer Stack)
- New creative in ads (refresh every 2-3 days)
- Different email angles each day
- Product spotlights, social proof, urgency tactics
- New product drops mid-sale (days 4-8)

## 6. BEYOND BLACK FRIDAY (December Strategy)

The period between BFCM end and Christmas is a significant revenue opportunity
that many brands neglect:

### Key tactics:
- **Free shipping / express shipping offers** for Christmas gifting
- **Gift guides** segmented by recipient or price point
- **Gift card campaigns** ("Don't know what to get them?")
- **Express shipping cutoff dates** create natural urgency
- **New product drops** - limited edition or Christmas-specific
- **Nurture new customers** acquired during BFCM

### What NOT to do:
- Don't immediately go on sale again (devalues the BFCM offer)
- Don't go silent - maintain communication cadence
- Don't ignore the new customers - they need a welcome/nurture sequence
- Don't run Boxing Day as a major event (demand already captured by BFCM)

## 7. EXECUTION FRAMEWORK

### Weekly Rhythm:
- Set day/time for product drops (consistency builds habitual buying)
- 2-3 email sends per week (minimum) during BAU periods
- Daily emails during sale events
- Social posting daily minimum
- Ad creative refresh weekly during BAU, every 2-3 days during sale

### Monthly Planning:
- Review demand plan and stock levels
- Plan next month's product drops and content themes
- Review upcoming events/occasions (2-3 months out)
- Assess need for any liquidation promos
- Review performance metrics vs annual targets

### Quarterly Planning:
- Review and adjust annual calendar
- Plan next revenue peak event (start 10-13 weeks before)
- Assess product development pipeline
- Review year-over-year comparisons

---

## 8. QUICK WIN TACTICS (Between Major Events)

When you don't have a sale or major drop planned, these tactics
keep revenue flowing:

1. **Back-in-stock alerts** - notify waitlisted customers
2. **Staff picks / founder favorites** - curated collections
3. **UGC spotlight** - feature customer content as social proof
4. **Educational content** - how to use/style your products
5. **Community moments** - behind the scenes, team content
6. **Cross-sell campaigns** - target past buyers with complementary products
7. **Loyalty program pushes** - if applicable
8. **Bundle offers** - create new value from existing products
9. **Referral campaigns** - leverage satisfied customers
10. **Content collaborations** - partner with complementary brands

## 9. KEY METRICS & BENCHMARKS

### Revenue Peaks Performance:
- BFCM: median 8x weeks revenue (2024 EE data)
- EOFY: typically 5-7x weeks revenue
- March sale: typically 4-6x weeks revenue
- 75% of brands hit biggest month ever during BFCM
- 72% hit most profitable month ever during BFCM

### Annual Revenue Distribution:
- November should be the single biggest month
- Q4 (Oct-Dec) typically represents 30-40% of annual revenue
- January/August typically the weakest months
- Three revenue peaks should noticeably lift the annual average

### Product Drop Performance:
- Consistent weekly drops outperform sporadic large collections
- 120 drops/year possible with the right cadence (I Love Ugly benchmark)
- Post-sale drops minimize revenue hangover by 20-40% (anecdotal)
- Mid-sale drops create measurable daily spikes in sale revenue

### Email Cadence Benchmarks:
- BAU: 2-3 sends per week minimum
- Sale events: daily sends (each with unique angle)
- New product drop: 1 email per drop + social push
- Event/gifting: 2-3 emails per occasion (teaser, launch, last chance)

## 10. THE ECOMMERCE EQUATION APPROACH

The Ecommerce Equation framework integrates the promotional rhythm
with the broader business operating system:

- **Weekly Demand Snapshot**: Monitor stock levels and plan replenishment
- **Cash Flow Accelerator**: ABC stock analysis guides promotional priorities
- **The Equation Sheet**: Track weekly revenue, orders, AOV, sessions, conversion
- **Sale Scorecard**: Capture quantitative + qualitative data from every sale event
- **7 Figure Sale Strategy Sheet**: Full sale planning workbook (11 tabs)
- **Media Plan Calculator**: Budget allocation tool for sale events
- **Pre-Sale Checklist**: Task management for sale preparation

The promotional rhythm sits on top of all of these as the strategic layer
that determines WHEN and WHY you activate each tool.
