## 1. THE SALE EVOLUTION (Maturity Model)

Brands progress through four stages of sale capability:

- **Amateur**: Ad hoc, wake-up-and-send-email approach. Typically 2-3x weeks revenue result
- **Rookie (Year 1 with EE)**: Follows the system with some blind faith. Tentative on spend. Usually 2-3x better than amateur
- **Seasoned (Year 2)**: Uses own data plus EE data. More confidence, optimizes the system. Reaches marketing potential but often hits stock/fulfillment issues
- **Pro (Year 3+)**: Clinical execution, maximizes entire system. Purchases stock specifically for sale. Uses demand planning. Pushes the next growth frontier

## 2. THE 11-BOX SALE STRATEGY FRAMEWORK

The core planning framework. Incorrect decisions cascade into compounding problems during sale.

### Box 1: Target Revenue
Three calculation methods:
- **Weeks Revenue Multiple**: Sale revenue = BAU weekly revenue x multiplier
  - Amateur: 2-3x | Basic tactics: 4-5x | Good: 6-7x | Great: 8x | Spectacular: 8+
  - 2023 median EE result: 6.75x | 2024 median: 8x
  - 70% of brands do 4.5-10x weeks revenue during sale period
- **Year-over-Year Growth**: Apply current YoY growth rate to last year's sale result
- **Stock Holding Ceiling**: Max target = stock at retail value x (1 - effective discount) x sell-through rate (typically ~50%)

Common mistakes:
- Cherry-picking a strong week rather than true average
- Being overly ambitious without data to support it
- Target too high leads to overspending on media with no return

### Box 2: Offer & Discount
Two primary discount types (94% of brands choose one):
- **Site-wide discount**: Best for narrow SKU range. Simple, honest message
- **Up to X% off**: Best for wide SKU range. Stronger marketing message, varied discounts by grade

Effective discount ranges:
- Maximization sale: 20-30% effective discount
- Liquidation sale: 30-40% effective discount
- 75% of brands land at 20-30% effective discount
- Consumers are now price-savvy - underlying discount matters more than headline

### Box 3: Effective Discount Calculator
Weighted average across stock grades:
- A-grade: lower discount (10-25%), but highest % of sales
- B-grade: moderate discount (30-40%)
- C-grade: deep discount (50-70%)
- Formula: Sum of (% of stock x discount x % of sales) = effective discount

### Box 4: The Enticing Offer Calculator
Ensures profitable sale orders using unit economics:
- Inputs: BAU AOV, weekly orders, product cost %, MER, shipping, fixed costs
- Sale inputs: effective discount, sale MER (typically 2/3 of BAU MER), GWP cost
- GWP (Gift With Purchase): landing cost should be ~7.5% of the minimum spend threshold
- GWP threshold: set ~25% above BAU AOV to lift average order value
- GWP uptake: typically 40-60% during sale
- Goal: positive profit per order during sale, not just revenue

### Box 5: Style of Sale
Based on returning customer rate:
- **Low repeat** (<20% returning): New customer acquisition focused
- **Hybrid** (20-40%): Mix of acquisition and retention
- **High repeat** (>40%): Existing customer base focused
- Style affects launch timing, hype spend, and offer structure

### Box 6: Sale Length
- Typical range: 7-14 days (some extend to 20)
- Longer sales need more mid-sale offers to maintain momentum
- "Get in, get the bag, get out" - avoid overstaying in competitive period

### Box 7-8: Launch Date & Hype Period
- Find the most advantageous time with "clear air" and less competition
- Earlier launch = less competition for ad inventory
- Australia BFCM: competition fierce in back half of November
- US market already so saturated that ads barely spend on actual Black Friday

### Box 9-10: Media Plan & Budget
- Sale MER typically 2/3 of BAU MER (more efficient due to offer)
- Budget = target revenue x sale MER
- Split by channel (typical: 90% Meta, 10% Google)

### Box 11: Stretch Target (A Bigger Bag)
Two paths to higher target:
- Option A: Hope for a higher weeks-revenue multiple (risky)
- Option B: Scale BAU revenue before sale via increased ad spend (more reliable)

## 3. SALE PHASES

### Phase 1: Pre-Sale Preparation (10+ weeks out)
- Complete 7 Figure Sale Strategy Sheet
- ABC stock analysis (Shopify > Analytics > ABC product analysis)
- Order GWP (gift with purchase) - 13 weeks lead time
- Build creative library and ad assets
- Set up sale campaigns in Meta (hype columns, spend limits)
- Prepare email/SMS sequences
- "Dashboard greenlit by Hype Day" - all prep done before hype starts

Pre-Sale Checklist categories:
1. Strategy (revenue target, offer, length, launch date)
2. Stock (ABC analysis, GWP ordering, demand planning)
3. Creative (ad creative, email assets, landing pages)
4. Meta Ads (campaign setup, audience building)
5. Website (sale pages, banners, discount codes)
6. Team & Training
7. Email & Customer Lifecycle
8. Google Ads
9. TikTok (optional)

### Phase 2: Hype (5-14 days before launch)
Purpose: Build anticipation, grow audiences, warm up ad accounts

Hype tactics:
- Email/SMS teasers with countdown
- Social media sneak peeks and giveaways
- Meta ads: Hype creative to warm audiences (MOF/BOF especially)
- Build email/SMS lists with early access signups
- Spending during hype should match strategy - overspending is a common and costly mistake

Important Meta settings during hype:
- Toggle OFF Advantage+ cost in MOF/BOF campaigns
- Untick "Use as suggestion" to keep audience targeting tight

### Phase 3: Launch (Day 1)
The 20-minute launch window:
- Have a set day/time (train your audience to expect it)
- Email blast at launch moment
- SMS blast 5-10 minutes after email
- Activate all sale campaigns simultaneously
- First hour/day is typically the biggest revenue spike

Email/SMS at launch:
- Plain text "founder email" from founder's name performs exceptionally well
- Subject line with urgency and offer clarity
- SMS should be short, direct, link to site

### Phase 4: Mid-Sale (Day 2 to Day N-2)
The mid-sale is where most brands lose momentum. Key strategies:

**The Offer Stack** (planned mid-sale offers):
- Design offers in advance with specific purposes:
  - Maximize profit (bundle deals, AOV boosters)
  - Clear stock (category-specific deeper discounts)
  - Create FOMO (flash sales, limited-time extras)
  - Re-engage (new product drops during sale)
- New arrivals launched mid-sale: release for 5 consecutive days (days 4-8)
  with 24-hour incentives on each to create daily spikes

**Mid-Sale Refresh** (Meta Ads):
- Refresh creative every 2-3 days
- Monitor frequency - if above 2.5, swap creative
- Scale winning campaigns, cut losers
- Add new ad creative from sale performance data (screenshots, UGC, testimonials)

**Mid-Sale Email Cadence**:
- Daily emails during sale (not spam - each with unique angle/offer)
- Mix of: sale reminder, product spotlight, GWP highlight, social proof, urgency/scarcity
- Plain text "personal" emails convert extremely well
- Segment by: purchased vs not purchased, opened vs not opened

### Phase 5: Sale End (Final 24-48 hours)
- "Last chance" urgency push across all channels
- Final email/SMS blast
- Consider extended hours or "surprise" extension for engaged non-buyers
- Meta: increase budget on best performers for final push

### Phase 6: Post-Sale
**Immediate (within 48 hours):**
- Complete Sale Scorecard - capture all data while fresh
- Quantitative: revenue, orders, AOV, ROAS, MER, email revenue %, profit
- Qualitative: what worked, what didn't, best ad creative, best email

**Post-Sale Recovery:**
- "Sale hangover" is real - revenue drops significantly
- Post-sale product drop: launch limited edition, collab, or standout new item
- Does NOT need heavy hype - use existing Advantage+ campaigns, refresh with new creative
- Product should be genuinely different (not just new colorway of what was on sale)
- Free shipping or upgraded express shipping offers work well heading into Christmas
- Nurture the flood of new customers acquired during sale

## 4. META ADS SALE CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE

Nine campaign templates for sale period:

**Top of Funnel (TOF):**
1. SALE-ASC-RS-TOF: Duplicate of best BAU Recommended Setup TOF
2. SALE-ASC-RS-TOF Combo: Excluding existing customers
3. SALE-ASC-RS-TOF-CIBS: Cost-Is-Bid-Strategy, excluding existing customers
4. SALE-NASC-TOM: No exclusions (broad)

**Mid Funnel (MOF):**
5. SALE-NASC-LS-MOF-All Mid: All mid-funnel audiences in one
6. SALE-NASC-LS-MOF-Split Mid (ABO): Individual ad sets per audience
7. SALE-NASC-LS-MOF-All Mid (ABO) ATC: Add-to-cart optimization

**Bottom of Funnel (BOF):**
8. SALE-NASC-LS-BOF-All: All bottom-funnel audiences
9. SALE-NASC-LS-BOF-All (ABO) ATC: Add-to-cart optimization

Key setup notes:
- Switch optimization from "Maximize Value" to "Maximize Number of Conversions"
- Use unique naming with "SALE-" prefix for easy identification
- Set spend limits to avoid overspending (critical)
- Ad scheduling available for precise launch timing
- Mid-sale: refresh creative, scale winners, cut underperformers

## 5. SALE TYPES ACROSS THE YEAR

### BFCM (Black Friday Cyber Monday) - November
- The biggest sale event. All boxes in the 11-box framework apply
- Black Friday has consumed Boxing Day demand - Boxing Day is now largely irrelevant
- Start hype early to find clear air before peak competition
- 75% of EE members have their biggest ever month in November
- 72% have their most profitable month ever
- Typical BFCM timeline: Hype starts 1-2 weeks before launch, sale runs 7-14 days

### EOFY (End of Financial Year) - June
- Second biggest sale event for AU/NZ brands
- Uses the same 7FSS framework adapted for EOFY context
- Good opportunity to clear winter/seasonal stock
- Often run 7-10 days
- Can be almost as strong as BFCM for some brands

### March Sale Event (Third Sale Event)
- Newest addition to the sale calendar
- Positioned as a "brand birthday," "Autumn sale," or stock clearance
- Shorter and lighter than BFCM/EOFY (5-7 days typical)
- Lower discount intensity, but good for post-summer clearance
- Helps smooth annual revenue curve - avoids deep trough between January and June

## 6. KEY METRICS & BENCHMARKS

### Sale Performance
- Weeks revenue multiple: median 8x (2024 EE data)
- 75% have biggest month ever in November
- 72% have most profitable month ever
- 99% followed EE processes, 96% followed strategy to a T
- 94% used site-wide or up-to-X% discount
- 94% felt their offer type was the best choice

### Effective Discount
- 75% of brands: 20-30% effective discount
- Maximization sales: 20-30%
- Liquidation sales: 30-50%

### Financial Targets
- Sale MER: typically 2/3 of BAU MER (e.g., BAU 17% -> Sale ~10-11%)
- GWP landed cost: ~7.5% of GWP minimum spend threshold
- GWP threshold: ~25% above BAU AOV
- GWP uptake: 40-60%
- Typical sell-through: ~50% of stock at retail value
- Stock holding sell-through above 60% = unlikely for fashion brands

### Email/SMS During Sale
- Daily emails during sale period (each with unique angle)
- Plain text founder emails consistently top performers
- Email revenue should be significant % of total sale revenue
- SMS: short, direct, link to site. Send 5-10 min after email

### Meta Ads During Sale
- Refresh creative every 2-3 days
- Frequency above 2.5 = swap creative
- BAU campaigns often paused or reduced during sale
- Sale-specific campaigns turned on at launch
- Budget: target revenue x sale MER, split by channel

## 7. THE SALE IDENTITY

Every sale needs a clear identity:
- **Sale name**: What are you calling it? (e.g., "The Big One," "Birthday Bash," "Stocktake Sale")
- **Visual identity**: Consistent colors, fonts, imagery across all channels
- **Messaging angle**: Why are you having this sale? What's the story?
- **Offer clarity**: Customer should understand the deal in 3 seconds
- **Creative consistency**: All ads, emails, landing pages should feel cohesive

## 8. PROFIT PROTECTION

### The Enticing Offer System
Core principle: discount to drive volume, but use unit economics to preserve margin:
1. Calculate profit per order at full price
2. Calculate profit per order at sale price (with discount)
3. Add GWP mechanics to lift AOV above BAU levels
4. Even with discount, higher AOV from GWP can deliver equal or better profit per order
5. Fixed costs spread across more orders = lower per-order fixed cost

### Pro-Level Stock Strategy
- Purchase specifically for Black Friday at advantageous margins
- Access factories with lower MOQ requirements due to large BF order volume
- Land product at 40-60% of normal cost
- Offer 50% discount while maintaining same gross profit
- This is what separates 7-figure sale days from average results

## 9. SALE SCORECARD (Post-Sale Data Capture)

Capture immediately after every sale (within 48 hours):

**Quantitative data:**
- Total revenue, orders, AOV
- ROAS by channel (Meta, Google, etc.)
- MER (marketing expense ratio)
- Email/SMS attributed revenue (campaigns vs flows)
- Profit per order, total profit
- Stock sell-through %
- GWP uptake %
- Best performing ad creative (ID, spend, ROAS)
- Best performing products (by conversions)
- Google Ads: ROAS, spend, revenue, conversions (exclude brand campaigns)

**Qualitative data:**
- What worked well
- What didn't work
- Key learnings for next sale
- Team/operational lessons

Store in the Sale Scorecard sheet for year-over-year comparison across EOFY, BFCM, March events.

## 10. COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Overestimating target**: Leads to overspending on media. Be accurate, not optimistic
2. **Spending too much on hype**: Extra hype spend above strategy rarely converts. One coach spent an extra $100K on hype with zero return
3. **Launching too late**: Competition for ad inventory increases dramatically in late November
4. **No mid-sale offers planned**: Revenue drops after day 1 without new reasons to buy
5. **Not capturing sale data**: Missing scorecard means repeating mistakes
6. **GWP price too high**: Kills conversion rate if perceived value doesn't match threshold
7. **Ignoring stock constraints**: Target must be achievable within stock holding
8. **Leaving prep too late**: "University assignment" approach creates stress and missed opportunities
9. **Not segmenting email sends**: Sending same message to buyers and non-buyers wastes opportunity
10. **Running Boxing Day sales**: Demand has shifted to November - Boxing Day is largely irrelevant for DTC
