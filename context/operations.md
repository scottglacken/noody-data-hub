# Operations & Productivity — Founder Context

> **Source:** Productivity Boost module from the Ecommerce Equation program. Focuses on founder capacity, time management, and operational efficiency for ecommerce operators.

---

## Core Principle: Founder Capacity Is the Bottleneck

Most ecommerce businesses under $5M are founder-led. The founder's time, energy, and focus directly constrain growth. Productivity isn't about doing more — it's about doing the right things.

**The 8-12 Hour Rule:** Dedicate 8-12 hours per week to growth work (the projects that move the needle). Everything else is maintenance. Most founders accidentally spend 90%+ of their time on maintenance (emails, customer service, operations) and wonder why growth stalls.

---

## 1. Start with Sleep

- 7+ hours is non-negotiable for decision quality
- Sleep-deprived founders make emotional, reactive decisions (Level 2 marketing behavior)
- Poor sleep compounds: bad decisions → more fires → more stress → worse sleep
- **Action:** Set a hard bedtime, no screens 30 min before, protect sleep like a business meeting

---

## 2. Win the Day — High Impact First

- Do your most important growth task in your first 90 minutes of work
- Before checking email, Slack, or social media
- Your cognitive peak is in the morning — don't waste it on admin
- **Sprints:** Work in 60-90 minute focused blocks, then take a genuine break
- **Action:** Identify your #1 growth task the night before. Do it first thing.

---

## 3. Win the Week — Quarterly → Weekly → Daily

The planning cascade that prevents drift:

1. **Quarterly Rocks:** 1-2 major projects from your 6-Week Game Plan (address your current constraint)
2. **Weekly Deliverables:** Break rocks into specific tasks for this week (8-12 hrs total)
3. **Daily Calendar Blocking:** Schedule growth tasks in peak energy hours
4. **Friday Review:** Did you complete growth tasks? What slipped? What's next week's priority?

**The trap:** Urgent operational tasks (support tickets, supplier issues, app bugs) crowd out important growth work. Calendar blocking protects growth time.

---

## 4. Avoid Multi-Tasking

- Context switching costs 20-40% of productive time
- Every time you switch tasks, it takes 15-25 minutes to regain deep focus
- Batch similar tasks together (all emails at once, all creative review at once)
- **Single-tasking** is the fastest way to complete work — one thing at a time, fully
- **Action:** Close all tabs and apps not related to your current task. Use "Do Not Disturb" mode.

---

## 5. Master Your Messages

- Email, Slack, DMs are the #1 time sink for founders
- **Batch processing:** Check messages 2-3 times per day at set times (e.g., 9am, 12pm, 4pm)
- Do NOT leave email/Slack open all day — it creates a reactive, interrupt-driven workday
- Set expectations with your team: "I respond within 4 hours, not 4 minutes"
- **Action:** Turn off all push notifications for email and messaging apps during focus blocks

---

## 6. Filter Interruptions

Every interruption that reaches you costs 15-25 minutes of recovery time. Use a three-filter system:

1. **Deny:** "This isn't something I should be involved in" — redirect to the right person or resource
2. **Delegate:** "Someone else can handle this" — pass to team member with clear instructions
3. **Defer:** "I'll handle this, but not now" — add to your batch processing time

**The test:** Before responding to any interruption, ask: "Is this more important than the growth task I'm currently working on?" If no, it waits.

---

## 7. Know Your Worth — Delegate Below Your Value

Calculate your effective hourly rate: Annual Revenue / 2,000 hours = your hourly value.

- At $500K revenue: ~$250/hr
- At $1M revenue: ~$500/hr
- At $2M revenue: ~$1,000/hr

**Any task you can pay someone <$50/hr to do is costing you money when you do it yourself.** This includes: packing orders, basic customer service, social media scheduling, bookkeeping, data entry.

**Action:** List every task you do in a week. Mark each as above or below your hourly rate. Delegate or automate everything below.

---

## 8. Manage Your Meetings

- Default meeting length: 15 minutes (not 30 or 60)
- Every meeting needs an agenda and a decision to be made
- "This could have been an email" is usually true — challenge every recurring meeting
- Batch meetings on specific days (e.g., Tues/Thurs) to protect deep work days
- **Standing meetings:** Keep to 10 minutes max, literally stand up

---

## 9. Just Say No — The VIP List

Most founders say yes to everything: new opportunities, partnerships, features, meetings, favors.

**The VIP List:** Define the 3-5 people/priorities that matter most right now. Everything else gets a polite no (or "not right now").

Saying no to good opportunities is how you say yes to great ones. Every yes to something unaligned is a no to your growth work.

---

## 10. Calm Your Calendar — Schedule by Design

Design your ideal week before it happens:

- **Monday:** Planning, review last week's data, set this week's priorities
- **Tues-Thurs:** Deep growth work in morning blocks, batch operations in afternoons
- **Friday:** Review, wrap-up, plan next week, team check-ins

### Key Principles
- Block growth work first, then fit maintenance around it
- Protect 2-3 "maker days" per week with no meetings
- Schedule breaks and personal time — burnout is a business risk
- Review and adjust the template quarterly as the business evolves

## Benchmarks & Metrics for Founder Productivity

| Metric | Target | Warning |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Growth work hours/week | 8-12 hrs | <4 hrs |
| Tasks delegated/week | 5+ | 0-1 |
| Message check frequency | 2-3x/day | Constant |
| Meeting hours/week | <5 hrs | >10 hrs |
| Sleep hours/night | 7+ hrs | <6 hrs |

### LER (Labour Efficiency Ratio)
LER = Total Revenue / Total Labour Costs (wages + contractor fees)
- **Target:** $2.00+ (every $1 of labour generates $2+ of revenue)
- **Warning:** $1.50-2.00
- **Critical:** <$1.50 — you're overstaffed for your revenue, or founder is doing low-value work

---

## The Compound Effect

These habits compound. A founder who:
- Sleeps 7+ hours (better decisions)
- Does growth work first (moves the needle daily)
- Batches messages (3+ hours saved/week)
- Delegates below their value (10+ hours freed/week)
- Says no to distractions (protects focus)

...will outperform a founder working 80-hour weeks in reactive mode. It's not about working more — it's about working on the right things with full cognitive capacity.
