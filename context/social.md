## Organic Growth Tactics (from Ecommerce Equation — Audience Growth Engine)

### Funnel-First Thinking
The game is won before the sale starts. The goal is to build the biggest, most engaged MOF/BOF audience possible BEFORE peak sale events. This means:
- Invest in audience building when media is cheap (Jan-Feb, Jul-Aug)
- Build the "trifecta": get customers on your pixel + email list + SMS list
- Every touchpoint should move people deeper into the funnel
- You reap what you sow — more seeds planted = bigger harvest at sale time

### Audience Building Tactics (Priority Order)
1. **Competitions/Giveaways** — fastest list-building tactic (details below)
2. **Brand Collaborations** — partner with complementary brands to cross-pollinate audiences
3. **Influencer Partnerships** — micro-influencers in skincare/parenting niche
4. **Organic Social** — consistent posting with engagement-first mindset
5. **TOF Ads** — broad/advantage+ campaigns to find new cold prospects

### Competition & Giveaway Strategy
Competitions are the #1 email/SMS acquisition tactic for DTC ecom. Run 2-4 per year.

**Two Types:**
1. **Traditional Giveaway (Gleam-style)**: Big single prize, defined draw date. Pros: big incentive, cross-pollination (IG follow + email + SMS). Cons: delayed purchasing (people wait to see if they win), sweepstakes scrapers bring junk emails, Gleam increasingly janky.
2. **Instant Win (preferred)**: Multiple smaller prizes, instant result like a poker machine. Pros: no delayed purchasing, can run for weeks/months, often delivers 2-4x ROAS during comp period, higher email quality. Cons: higher cost per email, less cross-pollination.

**Instant Win Setup (Klaviyo):**
- Create dedicated Klaviyo list (single opt-in)
- Build embedded form on Shopify landing page (first name, email, phone)
- Klaviyo flow with random sample split: 99% get runner-up email (consolation prize e.g. 10% off), 1% get winner email
- Success screen says "check your inbox to see if you're a winner"
- Direct ads to the landing page with purchase objective

**Prize Design:**
- Always give away YOUR product (not cash/Apple/generic prizes) — people who want your stuff are your future buyers
- Target <$0.50 cost of prize per email entry
- Formula: product cost / odds = cost per email (e.g. $20 product at 1-in-100 = $0.20/email)
- If odds are good (1 in 100) — lead with odds as marketing message
- If odds are worse (1 in 500+) — lead with "win one of X" messaging instead
- Consolation prize: slightly better than normal opt-in offer (e.g. 15% off vs standard 10%)

**Ad Setup for Competitions:**
- Use purchase objective (not traffic/landing page views) — 3x better email quality and ROAS
- Combo campaign structure: mid-funnel + broad-narrowed + broad in one campaign
- Advantage Plus also works well for competitions
- 4-8 ads per campaign, mix of flat lay, lifestyle, founder video
- Do NOT exclude existing customers — they cross-pollinate to SMS, refer friends, and are most likely to convert on consolation prize
- Avoid "win" wording in ads (gets flagged as gambling) — use "giveaway" or "free" instead
- Start small budget, measure cost per lead daily, scale what works

**Spam Prevention:**
- Filter emails containing "+" symbol in Klaviyo flow (common spam signature)
- For winner emails: require reply with full name/phone before sending prize coupon
- Use dynamic coupons for runner-up to prevent discount site leakage

**For Noody specifically:** An instant win competition giving away a Noody skincare bundle (cost ~$25-40) at 1-in-100 odds would cost ~$0.25-0.40 per email. Consolation prize: 15% off first order. This builds the email list for BFCM/seasonal pushes while maintaining positive ROAS.

### Reel Boosting Strategy
- Boosting reels from the Instagram app is now a legitimate growth tactic (unlike boosting static posts which is outdated)
- Use "profile visits" objective for cheapest follower growth
- Use "web visits" objective to drive to competition landing page or product page
- Budget: start with $5-10/day on best-performing organic reels
- Reels that get natural traction organically are the best candidates for boosting
- Can combine: boost the reel from app AND use the same video as an ad in Ads Manager

### Sell By Chat (DM Strategy)
- When someone follows your page, DM them personally: "Hey, thanks for following! Do you have any questions about [product]?"
- Position as genuine founder/customer service interaction, NOT a bot
- Ask unique personalised questions to build trust
- Can offer a time-limited coupon code to convert within 24 hours
- Laborious but effective for small brands — creates instant personal connection
- Especially powerful for Noody: parents of kids with eczema want to talk to a real person who understands

### Instagram Competition (Social-Only)
For quick engagement boosts between major competitions:
- "Like this post, follow us, tag a friend" format
- Smaller prize ($50-250 value)
- Run monthly or bi-monthly
- Creates 24-48hr halo effect of increased reach
- Good for new product launches or mid-sized releases
- No email/SMS capture but builds social following and engagement

### Content Strategy for Growth
- Treat content as the big picture — everything your brand says and shares across every platform
- Balance product-driven content with value-based and emotional content
- Think of your brand as a person in a conversation — all take and no give gets stale fast
- Content pillars should align with brand mission, customer avatars, AND business goals
- A healthy content mix fuels BAU performance outside of sale periods
- Businesses that struggle with BAU performance often lack content pillars beyond product and promotion

### Engagement Tactics
- Reply to every comment within 1 hour (signals to algorithm)
- Engage with 10-20 similar/complementary accounts daily
- Use relevant niche hashtags (eczema, sensitive skin, natural baby care)
- Share customer DMs/stories (with permission) as social proof
- Run polls and questions in Stories to drive interaction

### Dealing with Instagram Spam Accounts
When running competitions, fake accounts may impersonate your brand:
- They DM your followers claiming they "won" and try to skim credit card details
- Standard reporting through Instagram is slow/ineffective
- Instead: report via Facebook/Meta COPYRIGHT form (not spam form) — fake accounts steal your content/images
- This gets accounts taken down in ~3 days vs months through normal reporting
- Be prepared to verify identity (photo with driver's license may be required)
