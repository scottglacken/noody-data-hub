## 1. SEO Fundamentals for Ecommerce

### Why SEO Matters for DTC
- Organic search visitors convert 2-3x better than paid social (higher purchase intent)
- Organic search should drive 15-30% of total sessions for a healthy DTC brand
- SEO is a compounding investment — content ranks for months/years vs paid ads which stop when spend stops
- Reduces dependency on paid channels and improves overall MER

### Search Intent Types
| Intent | Description | Example | Page Type |
|--------|------------|---------|-----------|
| Transactional | Ready to buy | "buy eczema cream nz" | Product/collection page |
| Commercial | Comparing options | "best baby skincare nz" | Collection page or blog comparison |
| Informational | Seeking knowledge | "how to treat baby eczema" | Blog post |
| Navigational | Looking for specific brand/page | "noody skincare" | Homepage/brand page |

- **Transactional keywords** are highest value — target with product and collection pages
- **Informational keywords** drive top-of-funnel traffic — target with blog content
- **Branded search** should dominate your top queries; if not, brand awareness is the problem

## 2. Keyword Research Strategy

### Process
1. **Seed keywords**: List core product terms (eczema cream, baby skincare, natural moisturiser)
2. **Expand with tools**: Use Semrush or Google Search Console to find related terms, questions, long-tail variants
3. **Classify by intent**: Tag each keyword as transactional, commercial, or informational
4. **Prioritize**: Focus on transactional keywords with reasonable volume and achievable competition
5. **Map to pages**: Assign each target keyword to a specific page on your site

### Keyword Research Spreadsheet
Track per keyword: search volume, current position, target page URL, intent type, competition score, priority

### Using Google Search Console for Keyword Research
- GSC shows what Google already associates with your site
- Position 8-20 keywords = biggest opportunity zone (small improvements = page 1)
- High impressions + low CTR = title/meta rewrite opportunity
- Good position + low impressions = low volume, consider content expansion

### Semrush Workflow
- Filter by commercial/transactional intent for highest-value keywords
- Competitor gap analysis: find keywords competitors rank for that you don't
- Question-based keywords for blog content ideas

## 3. On-Site Optimization Checklist

### Schema Markup
- Structured data that helps Google understand your pages (Product, FAQ, Review, Organization)
- Enables rich snippets in search results (star ratings, price, availability)
- Shopify apps can auto-generate schema for product pages
- Test with Google's Rich Results Test tool

### XML Sitemaps
- Submit sitemap to Google Search Console
- Shopify auto-generates sitemap.xml at yourstore.com/sitemap.xml
- Ensures all pages are discoverable by Google
- Resubmit after major site changes (new collections, URL structure changes)

### Title Tags (Most Important On-Page Element)
- Format: **Primary Keyword | Secondary Keyword - Brand Name** (under 60 chars)
- Every page needs a unique title tag with target keyword
- Homepage: brand + value proposition; Collections: category keyword + brand
- Product pages: product name + key benefit + brand; Blog: topic keyword + brand

### Header Tags (H1, H2, H3)
- **H1**: One per page, should contain primary keyword. This is the page's main heading.
- **H2**: Section headings — use for major content sections
- **H3**: Sub-sections within H2 blocks
- Structure creates hierarchy that Google uses to understand page content
- Don't skip levels (H1 -> H3 without H2)
- Shopify: H1 is usually the page/product title; customize H2/H3 in description

### Meta Descriptions
- 150-160 characters; not a direct ranking factor but affects CTR (indirectly affects ranking)
- Include target keyword (Google bolds matches) + call-to-action ("Shop now", "Free shipping over $100")
- Write unique descriptions for every important page; Shopify: edit in page/product SEO section

### Internal Linking
- Link related pages to each other throughout your site
- Blog posts should link to relevant product/collection pages (and vice versa)
- Use descriptive anchor text (not "click here" — use "our eczema cream for babies")
- Helps Google discover and understand page relationships
- Distributes page authority across your site
- Aim for 3-5 internal links per blog post

### Image Optimization
- Descriptive file names (calm-balm-eczema-cream.jpg not IMG_4532.jpg) + keyword-rich alt text
- Compress images (TinyPNG/Shopify auto-compress); WebP format preferred. Large images = #1 page speed killer

### Video Optimization
- Host on YouTube/Vimeo and embed (don't self-host — kills page speed)
- Add video schema markup; include transcript text on page (Google reads text, not video)

## 4. Content Strategy for Ecommerce SEO

### Content as the Biggest SEO Lever
- Content (blog posts, guides, how-to articles) is the primary way to rank for informational keywords
- Drives top-of-funnel organic traffic that can be nurtured into customers
- Builds topical authority — Google sees your site as an expert in your niche

### Content Strategy Framework
1. **Identify questions your customers ask** (Semrush, Google "People Also Ask", customer support FAQs)
2. **Create comprehensive answers** as blog posts (1,500-3,000 words for competitive topics)
3. **Link to products** naturally within the content
4. **Optimize for featured snippets**: Use clear headings, bullet points, direct answers
5. **Update existing content** regularly — freshness matters

### Content Ideas for DTC Skincare
- "How to treat baby eczema naturally", "Best moisturiser for eczema NZ", "Eczema vs dry skin: how to tell the difference"
- Ingredient spotlights, seasonal guides ("Winter skincare routine for sensitive skin")
- Track performance by funnel stage: awareness (traffic), consideration (time on page), conversion (assisted conversions)

## 5. Technical SEO Checklist

### Core Web Vitals (Google Ranking Factor)
| Metric | What It Measures | Good | Needs Work | Poor |
|--------|-----------------|------|------------|------|
| **LCP** (Largest Contentful Paint) | Load speed of main content | <2.5s | 2.5-4s | >4s |
| **TBT** (Total Blocking Time) | Interactivity delay | <200ms | 200-600ms | >600ms |
| **CLS** (Cumulative Layout Shift) | Visual stability | <0.1 | 0.1-0.25 | >0.25 |

### Tools for Technical Audit
- **Google Search Console**: Core Web Vitals report, security issues, indexing status
- **GTmetrix**: Detailed performance breakdown with LCP, TBT, CLS scores
- **Semrush Site Audit**: Comprehensive crawl identifying broken links, duplicate content, missing tags, redirect chains

### Common Technical Issues (Shopify)
- Oversized images; too many apps loading JavaScript; render-blocking third-party scripts
- Duplicate content from Shopify URL structure (collection/product vs product URLs)
- Missing alt text; broken internal links after URL changes; redirect chains (A->B->C should be A->C)

### Technical SEO Maintenance Cadence
| Frequency | Task |
|-----------|------|
| Monthly | Check GSC for crawl errors, review Core Web Vitals |
| Quarterly | Run full site audit (Semrush), fix broken links, review page speed |
| After site changes | Resubmit sitemap, check for broken redirects, test new pages |

## 6. Next Gen SEO — Multi-Platform Search

### The Search Landscape is Fragmenting
People search across multiple platforms: **TikTok** (product discovery), **YouTube** (how-to, reviews), **Reddit** (authentic opinions, "best X for Y"), **ChatGPT/AI** (direct answers, comparisons), **Google** (still dominant for transactional but losing discovery share).

### Search Intent Changes by Platform and Journey Stage
| Journey Stage | Platform | Intent |
|--------------|----------|--------|
| Awareness | TikTok, Instagram | Discovery, inspiration |
| Research | YouTube, Reddit, Google | Comparison, reviews, how-to |
| Decision | Google, ChatGPT | Best price, where to buy, final validation |
| Purchase | Google, Direct | Transactional, brand search |

### Optimizing for AI Search
- AI models cite sources — make your content the most comprehensive, authoritative answer
- Structured content (clear headings, bullet points, direct answers) is more likely to be cited
- Build brand mentions across platforms (Reddit threads, YouTube reviews, blog posts)
- Ensure your product information is accurate and detailed across all touchpoints
- AI search rewards topical authority — the more quality content in your niche, the better
