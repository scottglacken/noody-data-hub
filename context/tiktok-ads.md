# TikTok Ads Context

Reference guide for TikTok advertising strategy, extracted from the Ecommerce Equation TikTok Ad Jumpstart program. Covers DTC ecommerce brands running paid TikTok alongside Meta/Google.

---

## Platform Fundamentals

- TikTok is less mature than Meta for attribution and optimization -- treat in-platform CPA benchmarks separately from Meta benchmarks
- TikTok does NOT reallocate budget effectively between creatives -- it backs a "winner" and keeps spending on it even after fatigue
- Creative burns out much faster than on Meta (often 5-14 days vs weeks)
- The platform penalizes stale creative by increasing costs rapidly
- TikTok is best used for top-of-funnel new customer acquisition, NOT remarketing (Meta does remarketing better)
- Use MER (marketing efficiency ratio) as the north star, not in-platform ROAS alone
- TikTok can drive indirect value: branded search lift, social followers, product awareness even when in-platform ROAS looks poor

---

## Budget Allocation

- Allocate ~10% of total ad budget to TikTok (70% Meta, 20% Google, 10% other incl. TikTok)
- Minimum viable daily spend: $50/day to start (view content phase)
- White belt (launch): ~$50-100/day across campaigns
- Orange belt (scaling): $150+/day minimum
- Calculate budget backwards from signal targets: need 50 signals/week; if cost per add-to-cart is $10, that is $500/week ($71/day)
- Do not start paid TikTok if you cannot sustain at least $50/day

### ABO vs CBO

- **CBO (Campaign Budget Optimization)**: Budget set at campaign level; TikTok decides allocation across ad sets. Best for beginners -- less control but simpler
- **ABO (Ad Set Budget Optimization)**: Budget set per ad set; you control allocation. Better once you have data on which audiences/creatives work
- Start with CBO, graduate to ABO for scaling once you know your winners
- Warning: CBO can over-index spend to underperforming ad sets; monitor and switch off poor performers manually

## Campaign Structure: The Belt System

### White Belt -- Launch Phase (Weeks 1-4)

Sequential campaign progression. Do NOT skip steps.

**Week 1: Community Interactions Campaign**
- Objective: Community Interactions (followers)
- Goal: ~1,000 followers (or a few hundred minimum)
- Purpose: Look like a real business, not a bot
- Structure: 1 campaign, 1 ad set (broad), 2-3 ads
- Budget: $50/day CBO
- Targeting: Broad, country-specific (e.g., Australia or NZ only), no age/gender restrictions
- Remove this campaign after moving to Week 2

**Week 2: View Content Campaign**
- Objective: Website Conversions > View Content (or Traffic > Landing Page View)
- Goal: 50+ signals in one week
- Purpose: Season the pixel, teach TikTok who engages with your brand
- Structure: 1 campaign, 1-2 ad sets (broad + optional interest), 3-4 ads per ad set
- Budget: $50/day CBO
- Placement: TikTok only (deselect other placements)
- Bid strategy: Maximum Delivery

**Week 3: Add to Cart Campaign**
- Objective: Website Conversions > Add to Cart
- Goal: 50+ signals in one week
- Purpose: Move pixel data closer to purchase intent
- Structure: 1 campaign, 1-2 ad sets, 3-4 ads per ad set
- Budget: $50-100/day
- Key insight: ATC campaigns often generate actual purchases on TikTok because the platform finds it easier to identify ATC-intent users than purchasers
- Remove Community Interactions campaign at this point

**Week 4: Complete Payment Campaign**
- Objective: Website Conversions > Complete Payment
- Goal: 50+ signals in one week, determine product-market fit for TikTok
- Structure: 1 campaign, broad + interest/behavior ad sets
- Budget: $60+/day per ad set (if ABO) or $100+/day (if CBO)
- Remove underperforming campaigns based on CPA
- This is the validation stage -- if you complete all four weeks with reasonable CPAs, TikTok works for your brand

### Orange Belt -- Scale Phase

Entry criteria: White belt complete, spending $150+/day, consistently hitting 50 signals/week, seeing ROI from conversion campaigns.

**Mission 1: Scale What Works**
- Add MORE campaigns (not more budget per campaign) -- scale horizontally
- Duplicate winning campaigns with different interest/behavior targeting
- Keep individual campaigns around $100-150/day max to mitigate burnout risk
- Do not introduce new campaign types yet -- stick with ATC + Complete Payment

**Mission 2: Creative Cadence**
- Set aside 1 day per month to create 8+ ads
- Always have a bank of 10+ creatives ready to rotate in
- Source creator/affiliate content for Spark Ads
- At $200-300/day spend, rotating ~10 ads/month is sufficient

**Mission 3: Use Platform Data**
- Analyze audience demographics (age, gender, interests) from TikTok analytics
- TikTok has built-in audience insight tools showing which behaviors/interests your buyers fall into
- Use data to inform creative angles and targeting
- Check which audiences are over/under-indexing and adjust creative to match

### Blue Belt+ -- Advanced (briefly referenced)
- Video Shopping Ads (TikTok's version of DPA)
- Product Sales campaigns linked to catalog
- Smart Performance Campaigns (TikTok's Advantage+ equivalent)
- TikTok Shop (US-based brands only currently; not yet viable for AU/NZ)
- Affiliate/creator programs at scale

## Recommended Active Campaign Count

- Run at least 2 campaigns simultaneously:
  1. A top-of-funnel campaign (view content OR add to cart -- whichever performed best)
  2. A complete payment campaign
- Brands that drop their upper-funnel campaign and only run purchase see CPA increase ~70% of the time
- The upper-funnel campaign feeds the pixel data that makes the purchase campaign efficient
- Think of campaigns as an ecosystem, not independent silos

---

## Targeting Strategy

- **Start broad** -- country-level, all genders, all ages
- Let TikTok identify who engages; then narrow based on data
- After initial broad testing, use TikTok's audience insights to find interest/behavior pockets
- Keep interest targeting and behavior targeting in SEPARATE ad sets (TikTok recommendation -- they optimize differently)
- Scale out with new interest/behavior ad sets rather than increasing budget on existing ones
- Do NOT use TikTok for remarketing at low budgets -- leave that to Meta
- Geographic: Focus on your primary market (single country) to condense signals; do not go worldwide early

---

## Ad Set & Ad Structure

- Per campaign: 2-3 ad sets maximum
- Per ad set: 3-4 creatives (not more -- TikTok won't distribute spend evenly across 5+)
- TikTok tends to pick one creative and dump all budget into it; fewer ads per ad set = more even distribution
- Rotate creatives across ad sets rather than always creating net-new
- When a creative burns out, swap it for a fresh one from your bank

## Creative Strategy

### Ad Formats
- **Spark Ads** (preferred): Run organic TikTok posts as paid ads. Can use your own posts or creator/influencer posts. Looks native, performs better
- **Manual Ads**: Upload video directly, create custom identity with brand logo. Less native feel
- Spark Ads will likely be your primary ad format

### What Works on TikTok
- Native, lo-fi content that looks like organic TikToks -- NOT polished studio ads
- Short duration: 15-30 seconds typical; match length to audience attention
- Hook in first 0-3 seconds is CRITICAL -- if you don't grab attention, they swipe
- Strong thumbnail (research shows thumbnail is the #1 reason people stop scrolling)
- Include a clear call-to-action but bring it forward (many viewers drop off after 5 seconds)
- Embody FaceTime energy -- talk to camera like you are chatting with a friend
- Fill the frame -- occupy at least 1/3 of screen, don't be tiny and distant
- Camera at eye level (peer-to-peer feel)
- Product should be the hero; do not let aesthetic backgrounds distract

### Creative Styles That Work
- Solve problem A with product B
- Get Ready With Me (GRWM)
- Unboxing / reveal
- POV scenarios
- Before/after transformations
- "You won't believe..." hooks
- Product demos in real settings (warehouse, kitchen, etc.)
- Switch and sell
- UGC-style content from real people using the product

### Hook Types (first 0-3 seconds)
- Ask a provocative question
- Make a bold/shocking statement
- Show an unexpected visual
- "Watch until the end" curiosity gap
- Address a pain point directly
- Pattern interrupt (unexpected scene change)

### Creative Refresh Cadence
- Check creative performance DAILY (5 min morning check)
- Refresh creative every 7-14 days at moderate spend
- At low spend ($50-100/day): ads may last ~2 weeks
- At higher spend ($300+/day): ads may burn out in 5-7 days
- Always have next batch of creatives ready BEFORE current ones fatigue
- Volume over perfection: produce more lo-fi ads faster rather than fewer polished ones

### Iterating Winners
- When an ad works, create 3-5 variations immediately (different hooks, different talent, different products)
- Speed is king: if something is trending/working, capitalize NOW before it burns out
- Spread winning creative across multiple campaigns/ad sets for maximum coverage
- Test same winning creative style with different audiences
- Test same winning audience with different creative angles

## Key Metrics & Benchmarks

### Columns to Set Up in TikTok Ads Manager
- Budget, Total Cost, Cost Per Click, Clicks, Click-Through Rate
- CPM, Impressions
- Conversions (note: tied to campaign objective -- ATC campaign shows ATC as conversion)
- Cost Per Conversion (CPA)
- ROAS / Return on Investment
- Total Complete Payments, Cost Per Complete Payment
- Complete Payment Rate
- Total Add to Carts, Cost Per Add to Cart
- Total View Contents, Cost Per View Content
- Reach, Cost Per Reach, Frequency
- Video Views, Video Views at 25%, Video Views at 50%, Average Watch Time

### Benchmark Thresholds
- Cost per add to cart: Aim for roughly half your target CPA (e.g., if CPA target is $30, ATC target ~$10-15)
- Cost per view content: Should be a few dollars for most ecommerce brands
- TikTok CPA is typically 20-30% higher or lower than Meta CPA depending on the brand -- establish your own TikTok-specific benchmark
- CPA at 3-4x your ideal range = creative is burnt out, switch it off
- Frequency: Keep low for TOF campaigns; high frequency = penalization and burnout

### Creative Burnout Signals
1. Budget concentrating on one creative that is progressively getting more expensive
2. CPA rising to 3-4x your ideal range over 2-3 days
3. Cost per add to cart spiking relative to other creatives in the same ad set
4. Click-through rate declining day-over-day
5. All metrics trending worse when comparing recent days to prior period

### Optimization Workflow
1. Campaign level: Compare performance across campaigns, identify where budget is going
2. Ad set level: Compare audiences, check if budget is going to underperformers
3. Ad level: Identify which creatives are fatiguing vs. which have headroom
4. Use comparison date ranges to spot trends (getting better vs. worse)
5. Switch off fatigued creatives, rotate in fresh ones from your bank

## Troubleshooting

### CPAs Increasing
1. **Creative**: Is creative fatiguing? Is the hook strong enough? Is there a clear CTA? Are you following a framework?
2. **Structure/Audience**: Is the pixel API-connected? Did you season the pixel properly? Are you running 2+ campaigns? Are there new audiences to test?
3. **Product**: Does the product have market fit for TikTok? Is it seasonal? Is inventory stocked? Is price a barrier (test a TikTok-specific promo)?

### Spending But Not Making Money
1. Does TikTok complement overall business strategy? (Check MER, not just in-platform ROAS)
2. Is everything set up correctly? (Pixel API integration, correct pixel ID)
3. Are you allocating spend wisely? (Budget going to right campaigns/ad sets)
4. Are you switching off burnt-out creative fast enough?
5. Are you remaining curious and iterating? (Not set-and-forget)

### When to Turn Off a Campaign
Before killing a campaign, verify:
- Have you refreshed creative at least twice?
- Have you tested different audiences (broad, interest, behavior)?
- Has the campaign been running at least 4 weeks?
- Did performance suffer when other supporting campaigns were removed?
- Have you checked omnichannel impact (MER, branded search, followers)?
- If all optimization levers have been pulled and it is still underperforming, then turn it off

## Pre-Launch Checklist

1. Set up TikTok Business Center and ad account
2. Install TikTok pixel via Shopify API integration (not just basic -- use API for best data)
3. Download TikTok Pixel Helper browser extension to verify pixel is firing
4. Post 8+ organic videos on your TikTok page
5. Understand what creative styles get traction organically
6. Have 10-15 creatives banked and ready for paid
7. Calculate your TikTok budget (10% of total ad spend, minimum $50/day)
8. Plan your white belt 4-week campaign progression

## Key Differences: TikTok vs Meta

| Factor | TikTok | Meta |
|---|---|---|
| Attribution | Less refined, establish own benchmarks | More mature, trusted in-platform data |
| Budget allocation | Does NOT redistribute well -- backs one winner | Better at distributing across creatives |
| Creative lifespan | 5-14 days typical | Weeks to months |
| Scaling approach | Scale horizontally (more campaigns) | Can scale vertically (increase budget) |
| Remarketing | Not recommended at low budget | Strong remarketing capabilities |
| Creative style | Native, lo-fi, UGC-style | Broader range of formats work |
| Management cadence | Check daily, cannot set-and-forget | Can be more hands-off |
| Budget increase | Can increase >20% daily if working | 20% daily increase rule |
| Audience data | Rich behavior/interest/hashtag data | Restricted by privacy regulations |
