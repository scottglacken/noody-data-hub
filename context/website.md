# Website Performance — Noody Skincare Context

## RPV: The Grand Equalizer
Revenue Per Visit (RPV) = Conversion Rate × AOV

RPV is the single most important website metric in the Ecommerce Equation. It tells you how much revenue each visitor generates. You can't control Meta's CPM or CPC, but you CAN control what happens after the click.

**Why RPV matters more than traffic:**
- Higher RPV means every ad click is worth more
- RPV improvements compound: 10% CR lift + 10% AOV lift = 21% RPV lift
- A $1 improvement in RPV is worth more than a $1 decrease in CPA
- RPV directly reduces effective MER

## Key Conversion Levers (Priority Order)
1. **Product page optimization**: Clear hero image, benefits-first copy, social proof, trust badges
2. **Site speed**: Every 1-second delay reduces conversion by 7%
3. **Mobile experience**: 70%+ of traffic is mobile — design mobile-first
4. **Social proof**: Reviews, UGC, testimonials, "X people bought this today"
5. **Trust signals**: Money-back guarantee, secure checkout badges, clear returns policy
6. **Simplified checkout**: Fewer steps, guest checkout, auto-fill, multiple payment options
7. **Cart recovery**: Exit-intent popups, abandoned cart emails
8. **Free shipping threshold**: Set ~$10 above current AOV to drive larger orders

## AOV Optimization Strategies
Every $10 AOV increase drops DTC MER by ~2-3 percentage points:
- **Bundles**: Product bundles at 10-20% discount vs individual (biggest lever)
- **Upsells**: Post-add-to-cart recommendations
- **Cross-sells**: Complementary products on PDP and in cart drawer
- **Free shipping threshold**: Set strategically to drive larger orders
- **Gift with purchase**: Add value without discounting (protects brand)
- **Tiered pricing**: Spend $X get Y% off

## New vs Returning Customers
| Metric | New | Returning |
|--------|-----|-----------|
| Typical CR | 1-2% | 3-8% |
| Typical AOV | Base | 1.5x base |
| Typical RPV | Base | 3-5x base |
| Healthy mix | 40-60% new | 40-60% returning |

Too much new = not retaining customers (leaky bucket)
Too much returning = not growing (stagnant acquisition)

## Traffic Source RPV Hierarchy (Typical)
| Source | Typical RPV | Why |
|--------|------------|-----|
| Email | Highest | Warm audience, high intent |
| Paid Search | High | Active problem-seekers |
| Organic Search | Medium-High | Intent-driven |
| Direct | Medium | Mix of brand loyalists + unknown |
| Paid Social | Medium-Low | Interruption marketing |
| Organic Social | Lowest | Browsing, not buying |

Invest more in channels with highest RPV — they're the most efficient.
