# Winning Demand Plan — Ecommerce Equation Framework

> Source: The Winning Demand Plan module from the Ecommerce Equation program. Covers inventory planning, cash flow forecasting, stock management, supplier relations, and demand signals.

## 1. Cash Flow is Oxygen

### The Cash Conversion Cycle
The time between paying for stock and receiving payment from customers:
- **Pay supplier** (deposit) → **Receive stock** → **Sell product** → **Receive payment**
- Shorter cycle = healthier cash flow
- Long cycles kill growing businesses — you're funding growth from cash reserves

### Cash Flow Killers in Ecommerce
- **Inventory prepayment**: Paying for stock 3-6 months before selling it
- **Seasonal loading**: Heavy stock investment before peak season
- **Ad spend timing**: Paying for ads today, revenue arrives 3-30 days later
- **Wholesale payment terms**: Net 30-60 from wholesale customers
- **Dead stock**: Money tied up in products that won't sell

### Cash Flow Best Practices
- Keep 3 months of fixed costs in cash reserve
- Separate operating account from growth investment
- Track cash flow weekly, not just monthly
- Build a rolling cash flow forecast (see below)

## 2. The Cash Flow Forecast

### Structure
- 12-week rolling forecast updated weekly
- **Inflows**: DTC revenue, wholesale payments, other income
- **Outflows**: Stock purchases, ad spend, fixed costs, tax obligations
- **Balance**: Running cash position week by week

### Key Inputs
- Historical revenue patterns (seasonality)
- Planned promotional calendar (sale events boost inflows)
- Upcoming stock orders (deposits and balance payments)
- Known fixed cost commitments
- Tax payment dates

### Early Warning Signals
- Cash balance trending toward zero within 4-6 weeks
- Large stock payment due during a known low-revenue period
- Multiple outflows clustering in the same week
- Wholesale receivables overdue (cash delayed)

## 3. The Weekly Demand Snapshot

### How It Works
Import Shopify sales data and current on-hand inventory to calculate:
- **Daily sales rate** per product (units/day average)
- **Weeks cover**: Current stock / daily sales rate = how many weeks until stockout
- **Reorder gap**: Lead time vs weeks cover — when to place the next order
- **Best sellers**: Products moving fastest (protect stock levels)
- **Slow movers**: Products sitting too long (candidates for clearance or bundling)
- **Potential stockouts**: Products that will run out before new stock arrives

### Reorder Point Formula
- **Reorder Point** = Average daily units sold × Lead time (days) + Safety stock
- **Safety stock**: 20-30% buffer for popular items
- **Seasonal adjustment**: 2x stock for peak seasons (BFCM, winter for skincare)

### Stock-Out Cost
Every day a hero product is out of stock costs you:
- Lost direct revenue
- Wasted ad spend (driving traffic to OOS product)
- Customer disappointment (may not return)
- Algorithm penalty (Meta/Google learn from conversion failures)
- **Rule**: Never let hero products go OOS. This is the #1 preventable revenue loss.

## 4. A/B/C Grade Stock Classification

### Grade A (Stars) — Top 20% of SKUs
- Drive 60-80% of revenue
- Highest turnover rate
- **Action**: Never run out. Reorder early. Allocate premium shelf/warehouse space.
- Monitor weekly

### Grade B (Workhorses) — Next 30% of SKUs
- Steady sellers, moderate turnover
- **Action**: Maintain steady stock levels. Reorder on schedule.
- Monitor bi-weekly

### Grade C (Slow Movers) — Bottom 50% of SKUs
- Low turnover, high carrying cost
- **Action**: Clear through sales, bundles, or GWP. Reduce reorder quantities.
- Consider discontinuing if not improving
- **Dead stock rule**: If it hasn't sold in 90 days at full price, it's a cash trap

### Moving Dead Stock
- Bundle with A-grade products
- Use as GWP (gift with purchase) to add value without discounting
- Flash sale to clear (accept the margin hit to free up cash)
- Donate for tax benefit (if applicable)
- Last resort: liquidate at deep discount to recover some cash

## 5. Supplier Strategy

### Get It Cheaper
- **Shop around**: Test multiple factories, don't settle for the first quote
- **Trade fairs**: Discover new suppliers, compare capabilities
- **Play factories off each other**: Use competing quotes for leverage
- **Bulk raw materials**: Buy fabric/ingredients in bulk for volume discounts
- **Volume incentives**: Higher quantities = lower per-unit cost

### Pay for It Later
- Start: 30% deposit, 70% FOB (standard terms)
- Negotiate toward: 30% deposit, 70% post-shipment or after goods land
- Some suppliers accept paying a small premium for extended terms
- Better payment terms free up capital for growth without external debt
- Build relationship over time to earn better terms

### Reduce Risk
- **Lower MOQs**: Use stock fabrics/standard formulations instead of custom
- **Factory templates**: Leverage existing designs to reduce minimums
- **Flexible POs**: Negotiate ability to adjust size curves, variants, or cancel portions
- **Monitor sales data**: Adjust POs based on real-time sell-through
- **Stage production**: Order in tranches rather than one large PO

### Influence Suppliers
- Build genuine relationships — visit factories when possible
- Pay on time consistently to build trust
- Give forward visibility on upcoming orders
- Be a good partner: clear communication, realistic timelines, fair negotiations

## 6. Sell It Faster

### Keep Momentum
- **Regular drops**: New products/colorways keep customers engaged
- **Pre-sales**: Pull demand forward, validate before committing to full production
- **Double down on winners**: When something sells, reorder immediately, don't wait

### Move Slow Items
- Bundle with best sellers
- Feature in email campaigns (dedicated send to relevant segment)
- Discount strategically (flash sale, not permanent markdown)
- GWP: Use slow movers as free gifts to add value

### Stock Planning Cadence
- **Weekly**: Review demand snapshot, check weeks cover for A-grade items
- **Monthly**: Review full inventory, identify B/C items trending down
- **Quarterly**: Full inventory audit, dead stock clearance, supplier review
- **Annually**: Full range plan aligned with promotional calendar

## 7. Funding Growth Without Debt

### 5 Ways to Fund Growth (Before Borrowing)
1. **Improve cash conversion cycle**: Faster turns, better payment terms
2. **Pre-sell**: Validate demand and collect cash before committing to production
3. **Supplier payment terms**: Negotiate extended terms (pay after landing/selling)
4. **Revenue-based financing**: Only if ROI is clear and immediate
5. **Cut dead stock**: Free up cash trapped in slow movers

### When Debt Makes Sense
- Clear ROI calculation (e.g. stock purchase for confirmed sale event)
- Short-term bridge (not ongoing operational funding)
- Terms are favorable and understood completely
- Can service repayments from existing cash flow

### Debt Traps to Avoid
- Cash flow lending with high interest rates
- Using debt to fund operational losses (fix the equation first)
- Multiple overlapping facilities creating compounding obligations
- Borrowing for inventory without clear sell-through projection
