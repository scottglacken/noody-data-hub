## 1. Program Overview

The Ecommerce Equation is a coaching program for ecommerce founders and marketers who want to take back control of their Meta (Facebook/Instagram) advertising. The program provides a structured roadmap from setting up ad account foundations through to scaling profitably.

**Program Roadmap Sequence:**

1. **Project A1 — The Ecommerce Equation:** Establish at least 3 months of data on the Clarity Sheet.
2. **The $100M Meta Ad Framework (Attract Module 1):** Build the right ad account structure tailored to your business.
3. **Project A2 — The Meta Ads Refresh System:** Learn to read and manage your account day-to-day.
4. **The Scale Challenge:** Test and implement scaling strategies to grow revenue and profit.

**Core Philosophy:** There is no single perfect ad account structure. Every ecommerce business is different (product count, AOV, target demographics, geography). The goal is to provide options, test what fits, and find a tailored structure that gives clarity, control, and confidence to grow.

## 2. The $100M Meta Ad Framework

### 2.1 Introduction & Philosophy

The $100M Meta Ads Framework helps you build a tailored ad account structure for your business. Instead of relying on agencies or guesswork, you set a strong foundation that supports scale.

**Key Principles:**

- No one cares about your business as much as you do — take ownership of your ad account.

#### 2.3.2 Lookalike Audiences

Built from custom audiences to find similar people. Select a source audience, choose a location, and set the audience size.

**Key Notes:**

- Standard size: 5% for Australia (most commonly used starting point).
- Value-based lookalikes use past purchase data and are typically better when your pixel has strong data.
- Each country needs a separate lookalike audience.
- Broad audiences now often act like lookalikes if the pixel is mature, but testing lookalikes is still worthwhile.
- If Value Purchase lookalike can't be created, skip it and revisit in 14–30 days once Meta has more data.

### 2.4 Build Campaigns (2025 Meta)

#### 2.4.1 The 3 Campaign Types

The three pillars that determine campaign type are **Budget**, **Audience**, and **Placements**.

| Term | Definition | Nature |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **ASC-RS** | Advantage+ Sales Campaign is toggled ON + Recommended Setup (Using Advantage+ Audience) | Let Meta decide on targeting |
| **ASC-LS** | Advantage+ Sales Campaign is toggled ON + Limited Setup (NOT Using Advantage+ Audience) | Make targeting suggestions |
| **NASC-LS** | Advantage+ Sales Campaign is toggled OFF. This is LS by default (NOT Using Advantage+ Audience) | Full control over targeting of the item toggled off (Budget, Audience, Placements) |

**Budget Options:**

- **Campaign Budget (Advantage+ Budget):** Meta spends an average of your daily budget but may overspend on good-performing days and underspend on others. Keeps the ASC toggle ON.
- **Ad Set Budget:** You control the exact spend. Toggling to ad set budget turns the ASC toggle OFF, making the campaign NASC.

**Audience Options:**

- **Recommended Setup (RS):** Meta controls targeting. You can provide suggestions (age, exclusions, interests) but they're treated as suggestions only.
- **Limited Setup (LS):** You have more flexibility and control over targeting options.

**Placement:** Typically left as Advantage+ Placements. Most brands (even those spending thousands per day) don't adjust placements.

#### 2.4.2 Campaign Naming Matrix

Format: `#[Number]-[Type]-[Setup]-[Funnel]-[Targeting]-[Exclusion Level]`

**Funnel Stages:**
- **TOF** — Top of the Funnel (cold prospecting)
- **TOM** — Top of Middle (Advantage+ Shopping Campaign / warm prospecting)
- **MOF** — Middle of the Funnel (retargeting)

### 3.5 Ad Assessment Scenarios

**Phase 1 vs Phase 2:**

| Phase | Duration | Date Range to Use |
|-------|----------|-------------------|
| Phase 1 | Ads running under 14 days | Full period (start date to today) |
| Phase 2 | Ads running over 14 days | Compare 14-day, 7-day, 4-day, and 3-day windows |

**Scenario 1 — Consistent CPA, Low Frequency (GREEN):**
- 14-day: $35 CPA, 1.2 frequency → Green
- 7-day: $40 CPA → Still green
- 3-4 day: $37 CPA → Green
- **Action:** You can SCALE.

**Scenario 2 — Good Start, Worsening CPA (YELLOW):**
- 14-day: $30 CPA, 1.2 frequency → Green
- 7-day: $60 CPA → Yellow
- 3-4 day: $75 CPA → Yellow/Red
- **Action:** OPTIMISE — look inside for ads burning out.

**Scenario 3 — High Frequency, Increasing CPA (RED):**
- 14-day: $40 CPA, 3.0 frequency → Red frequency
- 7-day: $70 CPA → Yellow
- 3-4 day: $110 CPA → Red
- **Action:** This is creative fatigue. REFRESH creative immediately.

### 3.11 Managing Expensive TOFs

Top of funnel campaigns naturally look more expensive because they reach many new people but only capture purchases within a 7-day click / 1-day view attribution window. Key insights:

**Why TOFs Look Expensive:**
- The pace of reaching new people is faster than the time people need to make a purchase decision.
- People exposed via TOF who don't convert within the attribution window may later convert via Advantage+ or MOF campaigns — making those campaigns look better at TOF's expense.
- Higher AOV = more expensive TOF CPAs (people need more time to decide on expensive items).

**Hero Ads in TOF:**
- Reach a lot of people (high reach)
- Sustain a low frequency in a 14-day window
- Maintain an acceptable (not necessarily the best) cost per purchase
- Example: Reaching 872K people at 1.5 frequency with an acceptable CPA

**How to Evaluate TOF:**
- Compare TOF campaigns against other TOF campaigns only (apples to apples).
- Accept that TOF will be more expensive — it's fuel for Advantage+ and MOF campaigns.
- Without TOF running, Advantage+ and MOF campaigns risk stalling.
- Prioritize spending on TOF detail interests that reach the most people at acceptable CPAs.

### 3.12 The Traffic Light System

An advanced benchmarking method to assess CPA, reach, and frequency across your account. Used daily to spot hero ads, identify burnout, and make clear optimisation decisions.

**Frequency Benchmarks (Universal — 14-day window):**

| Light | Frequency |
|-------|-----------|
| 🟢 Green | 1.0 |
| 🟡 Yellow | 2.0 |
| 🔴 Red | 3.0+ |

**CPA Benchmarks (Example — varies by account):**

| Light | CPA |
|-------|-----|
| 🟢 Green | $30 |
| 🟡 Yellow | $50 |
| 🔴 Red | $100 |

**How to Build Your Own Benchmarks:**

1. Look at your own ad account data across campaigns.
2. Identify the best (green), middle (yellow), and worst (red) performers.
3. Use those as your benchmarks — don't compare to other brands.

**Top of Funnel Traffic Light Example:**

| Light | CPA | Reach |
|-------|-----|-------|
| 🟢 Green | $31–$42 | 100K+ |
| 🟡 Yellow | $60–$80 | 50K–80K |
| 🔴 Red | $116+ | 20K–30K |

**Advantage+ Shopping Campaign Traffic Light Example:**

| Light | CPA | Reach |
|-------|-----|-------|
| 🟢 Green | ~$23 | 60K+ |
| 🟡 Yellow | ~$57 | 20K–30K |
| 🔴 Red | ~$81 | 5K–6K |

**Mid Funnel Campaign Traffic Light Example:**

| Light | CPA | Reach |
|-------|-----|-------|
| 🟢 Green | $9–$12 | 12K–30K |
| 🟡 Yellow | $20–$30 | ~3K |
| 🔴 Red | ~$48 | 1K–2K |

**Note:** Mid funnel frequency builds up much faster than TOF or Advantage+ campaigns.

**Ad Classification System:**

| Type | Definition | Characteristics |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Hero Ad** | The top performer | Highest reach, green frequency, acceptable CPA — sucks budget and sustains low frequency for longer |
| **Sidekick Ad** | Good CPA but limited scale | Low CPA but not scalable (lower reach, higher frequency) |
| **Psychic Ad** | Best CPA in the campaign | Lowest cost per purchase but may not reach as many people |
| **Turn Off** | Underperforming | Red CPA and poor reach/frequency — should be turned off |

## 4. The Meta Ads Optimisation Calculator (MOAC)

**Full Name:** Meta Ads Optimisation Calculator™

A tool that analyses your Meta ads using the "Marvel Math" classification system (Hero, Sidekick, Psychic ad types).

**Setup Inputs:**
- **AOV ($):** Your Average Order Value
- **MER (%):** Marketing Efficiency Ratio
- **Funnel Stage:** TOF, TOM, or MOF

**Toggle Options:**
- Conversion period: Regular vs Long
- Period type: Non-Sale vs Sale
- Metric mode: CPP (Cost per Purchase) vs ROAS

**Calculated Outputs:**
- Blended CPP (the ideal cost per purchase based on your inputs)
- Good CPP threshold (at or below this is good)
- Bad CPP threshold (at or above this is bad)
- Frequency range benchmarks

**How to Use:**
1. Enter your AOV, MER, and Funnel Stage.
2. Upload a **4-Day CSV export** (Cost per Purchase & Spend data).
3. Upload a **7-Day CSV export** (Frequency data).
4. Click "Analyse Ads" to get classifications and recommendations.

## 6. The Scale Challenge

### 6.1 Should I Scale?

A scale challenge tests whether your account can profitably handle increased spend. Signals you should scale include consistent green-light performance across your campaigns (low frequency, acceptable CPA, strong reach).

### 6.2 The Greenlight to Scale

**Checkpoints before scaling:**
- Campaign performance is consistently in green-light territory.
- You've established your own traffic light benchmarks.
- Your frequency is maintained under control in a 14-day window.
- CPA is stable across date range comparisons (14-day, 7-day, 3-4 day).

### Funnel Section Spend Benchmarks (Budget Allocation)

| Funnel Stage | Budget Allocation | Frequency Benchmark |
|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| TOF | 30–50% | ~1.0 |
| TOM | 30–40% | ~2.0 |
| MOF | 20–30% | ~3.0+ |

**Formula:** (Funnel Section Spend ÷ Total Spend) × 100 = %

### 6.3 The Scale Calculator

**Full Name:** The Scale Into Profit Calculator™

A tool that helps you plan a scaling timeline with daily spend projections, so you can model increased spend against projected profitability.

### 6.4 Did It Work?

After running a scale challenge, review whether the increased spend maintained acceptable CPA and frequency. If the scale test was profitable and sustainable, lock in the higher baseline.

### 6.5 My Scale Test Didn't Work

If scaling didn't produce the expected results, use a multi-pass troubleshooting process:

**1st Pass:**
- **Campaign Structure:** Ensure campaigns met scaling benchmarks for reach, frequency, and new customers.
- **Creative:** Generate more appealing creative to market to strangers and broader audiences.
- **Conversion:** Remove friction from website and ensure enticing offer.
- **Stock Levels:** Check if best-selling stock was depleted early during the scale period.
- Then run the scale test again. If it still didn't work...

**2nd Pass:**
- Repeat audit from 1st Pass.
- **Find New Niches:** Discover who else, and how else, your product could be used.
- **Expand Geos:** Use international expansion training to find and sell into new countries.
- Then run the scale test again. If it still didn't work...

**3rd Pass:**
- Repeat audit from previous passes.
- **Find New Channel:** Where else is your current niche buying, but with different buying behaviour?
- **Expand Product Range:** Create complimentary products to your hero/core products OR develop new hero/core products.
- Then run the scale test again.

Dedicated campaigns for testing new creative before rolling winners into your core campaign structure.
